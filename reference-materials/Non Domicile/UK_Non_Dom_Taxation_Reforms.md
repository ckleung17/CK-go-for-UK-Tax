# UK Non-Dom Taxation Reforms (Comprehensive)

## 2008 Reforms
- Introduction of Remittance Basis Charge
  - £30,000 RBC for long-term residents
  - Statutory requirements to claim remittance basis
- [Finance Act 2008](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2008/9/contents)

## 2017 Reforms
- Deemed Domicile Rule (15/20)
  - Deemed domiciled if resident 15 of previous 20 tax years
  - Applies to Income Tax, CGT, and IHT
- Trust Protections Introduced
  - Protected Trusts for non-settlor interested
  - Anti-avoidance via TPI and PFSI rules
- CGT Rebasing Relief
  - Available to non-doms becoming deemed domiciled in 2017
  - Rebasing to 5 April 2017 value
- [Finance Act 2017](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2017/10/contents/enacted)
- [HMRC RDRM](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/residence-domicile-and-remittance-basis)

## 2025 Reforms (Proposed)
- Abolition of Remittance Basis
  - Effective from 6 April 2025
  - Replaced by 4-year FIG regime
- 4-Year Foreign Income & Gains (FIG) Regime
  - [Definition](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/residence-based-taxation-draft-guidance): Exemption for new UK residents from UK tax on foreign income/gains not remitted
  - [Eligibility](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/residence-based-taxation-draft-guidance):
    - Must be UK tax resident for the relevant year
    - Must not have been UK resident in any of the 10 preceding tax years
    - Applies automatically for the first 4 tax years of UK residence
    - Available to post-6 April 2025 arrivals or earlier arrivals with 10-year non-residence
  - [Interaction with Trusts](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/residence-based-taxation-draft-guidance):
    - Income/gains in protected non-UK trusts sheltered if not remitted
    - PFSI and TPI matching rules continue to apply after FIG period
  - [Example 1: New Arrival](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/residence-based-taxation-draft-guidance):
    - Arrives 6 April 2025
    - No UK residence in past 10 years
    - Qualifies for FIG from 2025/26 to 2028/29
  - [Example 2: Returnee](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/residence-based-taxation-draft-guidance):
    - UK resident in 2024/25
    - Non-resident for 10 years prior
    - FIG applies from 2025/26 to 2028/29
- CGT Rebasing (Transitional)
  - Rebasing to 5 April 2017 for qualifying assets
  - Applies if remittance basis claimed since 2017/18 and held assets continuously
- Temporary Repatriation Facility (TRF)
  - Applies to 2025/26 and 2026/27
  - Allows remittance of pre-6 April 2025 foreign income/gains at 12% tax rate
  - Only for individuals not yet domiciled or deemed domiciled by 6 April 2025
- Overseas Workday Relief (OWR)
  - Still available post-2025
  - Limited to first 3 years of UK tax residence
  - Applies only to earnings from overseas duties
  - Only claimable if paid into non-UK bank account
- IHT Reforms (Residence-based)
  - 10-year rule for deemed domicile status
    - Becomes deemed domiciled after 10 consecutive UK tax years
    - Remains within scope for 10 tax years after departure
  - Reset rule
    - 6 years non-residence resets the 10-year clock
  - Worldwide assets within IHT scope after deemed domicile
- [HMRC March 2024 Policy Paper](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/residence-based-taxation-policy-paper)
- [Draft Guidance on Reforms](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/residence-based-taxation-draft-guidance)

## Anti-Avoidance and Trusts
- Settlor-Interested Trust Rules
  - Distributions may be matched with trust income/gains
- Protected Trusts
  - Only available to non-doms who set up trust before becoming deemed domiciled
  - Still available during FIG period if income/gains not remitted
- PFSI (Protected Foreign Source Income)
  - Income/gains in protected trust not taxed unless matched
- TPI (Taxable Potentially Income)
  - Matching applies on benefits/distributions
- FIG Interaction
  - During FIG: remitted trust income/gains taxable
  - After FIG: full matching applies
