Hold-over relief

Capital Gains Tax — deferring gains on gifts

HomeTrust Index

Tax treatment

Study checkpoint: 2026/27. Gift hold-over relief can defer rather than exempt a gain by reducing the recipient’s base cost. Trust transactions most often require comparison of section 260 TCGA 1992 for chargeable transfers and section 165 for qualifying business assets. Eligibility, exclusions and joint claim mechanics differ.

Compare section 260 with section 165, quantify the deferred amount, and record all applicable restrictions before completing a claim.

Section 260

Can apply where the disposal is a chargeable transfer for IHT purposes, even if no IHT is ultimately payable because of the nil-rate band or specified reliefs.

Practical issues and safeguards

A hold-over claim can affect later Private Residence Relief and other computations. Do not claim automatically without modelling the recipient’s future base cost, residence and intended asset use.

  • Calculate the unrelieved gain before testing each statutory hold-over route.
  • Check claimant, transferee, asset and excluded-property conditions and claim formalities.
  • Model the recipient’s reduced base cost and later disposal before choosing relief.
