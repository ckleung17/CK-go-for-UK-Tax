Interest-in-possession trusts

Current enjoyment and future capital

Overview

An interest-in-possession trust normally gives a beneficiary an immediate entitlement to trust income as it arises, or present enjoyment of particular property, while capital is preserved for another beneficiary. Administration and tax depend on the precise rights and creation date.

Identify the life tenant and remainderman, then trace the income administration and complete the separate IHT classification rather than relying on the trust’s label.

The detailed analysis begins with Life tenant, which provides the foundation for the concepts that follow.

How an interest-in-possession trust operates

This topic turns on four linked questions. Resolve them in sequence using the governing instrument, event chronology and supporting evidence, because the answer to one question can alter the next.

Life tenant

Often entitled to net income or occupation during life, without an absolute right to the underlying capital.

That conclusion leads to the next question: remainderman.

Remainderman

Holds the succeeding capital interest, subject to the prior interest and any powers in the instrument.

That conclusion leads to the next question: income administration.

Income administration

Trustees receive income, deduct properly chargeable expenses and account for the balance, unless income is validly mandated directly.

That conclusion leads to the next question: iht classification.

IHT classification

An interest created after 21 March 2006 is not automatically estate-linked; check whether it is an immediate post-death, disabled person’s or transitional serial interest.

Taken together, these concepts determine which records, checks and safeguards are needed in practice.

Practical issues and safeguards

A right to a fixed annual payment is not necessarily an interest in possession in all trust income. Read the charging language, powers and expense provisions rather than relying on “life interest” shorthand.

  • Read income, expense, accumulation, advancement and termination clauses together.
  • Separate the life tenant’s present right from the remainderman’s future interest.
  • Test the IHT category from creation circumstances and date, not the label life interest.

A will leaves a rental property on trust for Sam to receive net rents for life, with the property passing to Lee on Sam’s death. Trustees must manage the property and allocate expenses correctly; Sam’s IHT treatment depends on whether the interest meets the immediate post-death conditions.
