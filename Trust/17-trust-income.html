Tax treatment

Study checkpoint: 2026/27. Trustees must preserve the legal and tax character of receipts. Savings, dividends, rents and trading profits follow different computation rules. For 2026/27, accumulation and discretionary trustees generally face 45% on non-dividend income and 39.35% on dividend-type income, subject to the statutory de minimis rules and special cases.

For each tax year, complete the source computation, check the de minimis amount, apply the special trust rates, and maintain the tax pool where relevant.

Source computation

Compute each source under its own rules before combining trust income; capital receipts and sale proceeds are not income merely because cash is received.

Analyse the substance rather than the label. Read the trust instrument with the applicable legislation and current official guidance, identify the person whose rights or obligations are affected, and distinguish the trust-law answer from each tax and reporting question. The file should state the effective date, asset or amount involved and evidence supporting the conclusion. Once that position is recorded, it can be carried into the analysis of de minimis amount without assuming that the same legal or tax classification applies.

De minimis amount

HMRC guidance describes a normally £500 trust-income amount, divided across certain multiple settlements; if income exceeds the applicable amount, the exemption is lost rather than applied as an allowance.

Practical issues and safeguards

Do not deduct capital expenditure or private beneficiary costs as source expenses. Trust-management expenses have their own ordering and grossing rules and should not be confused with property or trading deductions.

  • Compute each income source before applying trust rates or management-expense rules.
  • Maintain the tax pool from tax actually available under the statutory rules.
  • Recheck current rates, de minimis treatment and beneficiary certificate requirements for the year.
