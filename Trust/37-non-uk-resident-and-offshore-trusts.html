Integrated framework

Study checkpoint: law and guidance checked to 17 July 2026. An offshore trust analysis requires parallel schedules for trustee residence, settlor residence and interest, beneficiary residence, income sources, gains pools, capital payments and IHT situs. From 6 April 2025 the FIG regime and residence-based IHT changed parts of the analysis but did not replace the underlying trust rules.

An offshore review keeps trustee residence, possible income attribution, later gain matching, and residence-based IHT in separate schedules before integrating the result.

The detailed analysis begins with Trustee residence, which provides the foundation for the concepts that follow.

Integrating an offshore trust analysis

This topic turns on four linked questions. Resolve them in sequence using the governing instrument, event chronology and supporting evidence, because the answer to one question can alter the next.

Trustee residence

Income Tax and CGT residence depends on trustee composition and, in some cases, the settlor’s status when the trust was created or funded.

That conclusion leads to the next question: income attribution.

Income attribution

Settlor-interested rules and transfer-of-assets-abroad provisions can charge UK residents on foreign trust income, subject to statutory conditions and defences.

That conclusion leads to the next question: gain matching.

Gain matching

Sections 86 and 87 TCGA require chronological gains and capital-payment records, including onward gifts and unmatched pools.

That conclusion leads to the next question: residence-based iht.

Residence-based IHT

Foreign settled property status follows long-term residence tests from 6 April 2025, with deceased-settlor and qualifying-interest exceptions.

Taken together, these concepts determine which records, checks and safeguards are needed in practice.

Practical issues and safeguards

“Offshore” does not mean exempt, and remitting cash is no longer the sole trigger after the remittance basis ended. Historic protected pools, pre-2025 income and gains, TRF designations and post-2025 amounts must remain distinguishable.

  • Maintain sterling pools separating pre-2025, transitional and later income and gains.
  • Classify residence, settlor interest, payments, loans, benefits and remittances for each year.
  • Test FIG, TRF, attribution, matching, IHT and international reporting independently.

Jersey trustees hold a global portfolio for a UK-resident settlor and adult children. In 2026/27 they realise gains, pay a beneficiary’s expense and lend to another. Applied analysis: Build a dated event ledger, value each item in sterling, apply settlor and beneficiary rules in order, update pools and separately review IHT and reporting.
