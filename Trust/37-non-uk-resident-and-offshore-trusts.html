Integrated framework

Study checkpoint: law and guidance checked to 17 July 2026. An offshore trust analysis requires parallel schedules for trustee residence, settlor residence and interest, beneficiary residence, income sources, gains pools, capital payments and IHT situs. From 6 April 2025 the FIG regime and residence-based IHT changed parts of the analysis but did not replace the underlying trust rules.

An offshore review keeps trustee residence, possible income attribution, later gain matching, and residence-based IHT in separate schedules before integrating the result.

Trustee residence

Income Tax and CGT residence depends on trustee composition and, in some cases, the settlor’s status when the trust was created or funded.

Analyse the substance rather than the label. Read the trust instrument with the applicable legislation and current official guidance, identify the person whose rights or obligations are affected, and distinguish the trust-law answer from each tax and reporting question. The file should state the effective date, asset or amount involved and evidence supporting the conclusion. Once that position is recorded, it can be carried into the analysis of income attribution without assuming that the same legal or tax classification applies.

Practical issues and safeguards

“Offshore” does not mean exempt, and remitting cash is no longer the sole trigger after the remittance basis ended. Historic protected pools, pre-2025 income and gains, TRF designations and post-2025 amounts must remain distinguishable.

  • Maintain sterling pools separating pre-2025, transitional and later income and gains.
  • Classify residence, settlor interest, payments, loans, benefits and remittances for each year.
  • Test FIG, TRF, attribution, matching, IHT and international reporting independently.
