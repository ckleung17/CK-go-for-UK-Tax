Operational framework

Study checkpoint: requirements current on 17 July 2026. The lead trustee coordinates the trust’s Self Assessment, but all trustees remain responsible. The SA900 return combines source income, gains, settlor information, beneficiary payments, tax pools and claims. It must reconcile to accounts, TRS and statements issued to taxpayers.

Compliance requires attention to any notice to file, the correct supplementary pages, all applicable deadlines, and the treatment of payment and claims.

Notice to file

A return is required when HMRC issues a notice; trustees also need to notify chargeability and register when the trust becomes liable.

Analyse the substance rather than the label. Read the trust instrument with the applicable legislation and current official guidance, identify the person whose rights or obligations are affected, and distinguish the trust-law answer from each tax and reporting question. The file should state the effective date, asset or amount involved and evidence supporting the conclusion. Once that position is recorded, it can be carried into the analysis of supplementary pages without assuming that the same legal or tax classification applies.

Practical issues and safeguards

The tax year printed on the latest available form may lag the current study year. Use the form and notes for the actual return year, and do not treat beneficiary statements as substitutes for trustee reporting.

  • Use the return, notes and supplementary pages for the actual tax year.
  • Reconcile source computations, gains, foreign items, payments and beneficiary certificates.
  • Maintain filing, amendment, payment and enquiry evidence in the annual file.
