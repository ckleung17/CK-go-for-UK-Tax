IHT framework

Study checkpoint: chargeable events in 2026/27. Certain trusts are excluded from the ordinary relevant-property regime or use modified charges because they satisfy detailed statutory conditions. These include disabled-person trusts, bereaved-minor trusts and age 18-to-25 trusts. Asset reliefs and charity exemption are separate and must be tested at each event.

Test separately for a bereaved-minor trust, an age 18-to-25 trust, a disabled-person trust, and any available asset reliefs; similar protective purposes do not make the statutory conditions interchangeable.

The detailed analysis begins with Bereaved-minor trust, which provides the foundation for the concepts that follow.

Testing special trusts and asset reliefs

This topic turns on four linked questions. Resolve them in sequence using the governing instrument, event chronology and supporting evidence, because the answer to one question can alter the next.

Bereaved-minor trust

Generally derives from a deceased parent’s will, intestacy or specified compensation arrangement and requires absolute entitlement by 18.

That conclusion leads to the next question: age 18-to-25 trust.

Age 18-to-25 trust

A qualifying will or intestacy trust can postpone absolute entitlement beyond 18 but applies a modified charge when property leaves before 25.

That conclusion leads to the next question: disabled-person trust.

Disabled-person trust

Special treatment depends on the statutory definition of disabled person and strict limits on benefit for others.

That conclusion leads to the next question: asset reliefs.

Asset reliefs

Agricultural property relief, business relief and charity exemption depend on current conditions, ownership periods, asset use and post-6 April 2026 limits.

Taken together, these concepts determine which records, checks and safeguards are needed in practice.

Practical issues and safeguards

From 6 April 2026, agricultural and business relief rules and limits require current verification. A trust category does not itself guarantee relief for the assets it holds.

  • Test every statutory condition for the beneficiary, source, age and vesting terms.
  • Apply current asset-specific APR or BPR conditions and limits separately.
  • Use the special charging formula and reporting rules rather than the ordinary regime by default.

The illustration below applies those safeguards to a concrete fact pattern and shows how the concepts work together.

Illustration

A deceased parent’s will holds assets for a child until 25. It cannot qualify as a bereaved-minor trust beyond 18, but may meet the age 18-to-25 conditions. Capital paid at 22 requires the modified statutory charge calculation rather than an ordinary ten-year charge.

Applied analysis: Verify the will and age conditions, classify the trust as age 18-to-25 if satisfied, and calculate the payment at 22 using the modified statutory regime.
