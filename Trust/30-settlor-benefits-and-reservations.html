IHT framework

Study checkpoint: chargeable events in 2026/27. A completed transfer into trust can remain relevant to the settlor’s death estate under the gifts-with-reservation rules if possession or enjoyment has not genuinely passed to the exclusion of the settlor. Separate Income Tax settlor-interested and pre-owned-assets rules may also apply.

The review connects any reservation of benefit, the settlor’s discretionary eligibility, the effect of cessation, and a possible POAT interaction without treating those regimes as substitutes.

Overlapping roles

A settlor who is also a trustee does not retain a benefit merely because they help administer the trust: trustee powers must be exercised in a fiduciary capacity under the deed. The analysis changes if the settlor can use the property, receive income or capital, borrow on favourable terms, direct benefits for themselves, or remain within a discretionary beneficiary class.

Being named as a beneficiary does not produce the same result under every tax code. Test the actual rights, powers and enjoyment separately under the gifts-with-reservation rules, the Income Tax settlements legislation, pre-owned-assets rules and the relevant trust charging regime. A role that is acceptable under trust law can still have significant tax consequences.

Record each transaction in the correct capacity. Trustee reimbursement of a properly incurred expense is different from a beneficiary distribution or settlor benefit, and the evidence should demonstrate the legal basis, amount and approval process.

Practical issues and safeguards

Paying market rent may help in some occupation cases but does not cure every reservation or discretionary-class problem. Analyse the deed, consideration, timing and actual enjoyment.

  • Map every direct, indirect and discretionary route by which the settlor can benefit.
  • Compare deed powers with actual occupation, payments, loans and expense records.
  • Test GWR, settlements legislation and POAT separately and record any cessation date.
