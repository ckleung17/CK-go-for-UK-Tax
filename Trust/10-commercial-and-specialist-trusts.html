Overview

Trust law supports many arrangements that are not ordinary family settlements. Employee benefit, pension, charitable, unit, security, client-money and co-ownership trusts operate within specialist regulatory and tax frameworks. Their commercial purpose and governing legislation must be identified before applying general trust rules.

The instrument and commercial purpose determine whether the arrangement is an employee benefit trust, pension trust, charitable trust, or a co-ownership or security trust, with different consequences following from each classification.

The detailed analysis begins with Employee benefit trust, which provides the foundation for the concepts that follow.

Distinguishing commercial and specialist trusts

This topic turns on four linked questions. Resolve them in sequence using the governing instrument, event chronology and supporting evidence, because the answer to one question can alter the next.

Employee benefit trust

Usually holds assets for employees or former employees; employment-income, Part 7A, corporation-tax and IHT rules may overlap.

That conclusion leads to the next question: pension trust.

Pension trust

Registered pension schemes have their own statutory tax framework and should not be analysed as an ordinary discretionary family trust.

That conclusion leads to the next question: charitable trust.

Charitable trust

Must satisfy charity-law requirements and tax conditions; charity registration and HMRC recognition are separate processes.

That conclusion leads to the next question: co-ownership or security trust.

Co-ownership or security trust

May hold title for owners, lenders or investors with rights principally defined by property or commercial documents.

Taken together, these concepts determine which records, checks and safeguards are needed in practice.

Practical issues and safeguards

A product name containing “trust” does not prove that ordinary trust taxation applies. Unit trusts, pension schemes and investment trusts can have materially different legal forms and statutory regimes.

  • Identify the legal and regulatory regime before ordinary trust taxation.
  • Map all employers, trustees, employees, operators, connected entities and benefits.
  • Check employment tax, corporation tax, securities, IHT and registration separately.
