Will and family trusts

Trust structures — succession and protection

HomeTrust Index

Overview

A will can transfer property outright or create continuing trusts after death. Before analysing a continuing trust, separate the role of the personal representatives, who administer the deceased’s estate, from the role of trustees, who hold ascertained property under the continuing terms. The same individuals may occupy both offices, but the legal capacity, accounts and tax treatment are not interchangeable.

Work through four questions:

  1. What remains within the administration period? Identify unascertained residue, liabilities, sales and estate income for which the personal representatives remain responsible.
  2. When and how does the continuing will trust operate? Read the will, assents and appropriations and record when each asset or fund becomes subject to continuing trusts.
  3. Does a beneficiary hold an immediate post-death interest? Apply the statutory conditions rather than relying on the expression “life interest”.
  4. Which rules protect younger beneficiaries? Distinguish an ordinary relevant-property trust from a qualifying bereaved-minor or age 18-to-25 trust.

The chronology therefore controls the analysis. Establishing what the personal representatives still need to do reveals the property available for the continuing trust; the trust terms then identify the beneficial interests, and those interests determine the IHT and registration questions.

From estate administration to continuing trusts

Administration period

The administration period starts on death and ends when the personal representatives have completed their administrative duties. They collect assets, establish debts and tax, pay expenses and legacies and ascertain the residue. Until residue is ascertained, a residuary beneficiary generally has a right to due administration rather than ownership of a specific estate investment.

Sales and income during this period belong in estate records. An asset does not become trust property merely because the will ultimately directs residue to trustees, and the date of grant is not automatically the end of administration. Completion may occur at different times for a specific legacy and the residuary fund.

Illustrative example: Daniel dies owning a portfolio and a business with unresolved tax liabilities. His will gives residue to trustees. Although the executors receive probate in September, they sell investments in December to meet liabilities. Those transactions remain part of estate administration until the net residue is established or assets are validly appropriated earlier.

Will trust

The will must be construed to identify whether a specific legacy trust starts at death, an express administration trust covers the estate immediately, or a residuary trust becomes operational only when residue is ascertained or assets are transferred. Record the moment at which each individual acts as personal representative or trustee and the assets held in each capacity.

Once operational, the trust instrument governs investment, occupation, income, maintenance, advancement and capital distribution. Estate accounts should close into opening trust accounts through a documented assent or appropriation, with acquisition values, income and liabilities reconciled. Income Tax, Capital Gains Tax and Trust Registration Service treatment should then be reviewed in the correct capacity.

Illustrative example: Daniel’s will leaves a named property on immediate trust for his spouse but places the residue on trust for children. The property trust may begin at death, while the residuary investment trust depends on administration and transfer. The trustees maintain separate opening dates and records rather than treating the will as creating one undifferentiated fund.

Immediate post-death interest

For IHT, an immediate post-death interest is a particular statutory category under IHTA 1984 section 49A. Broadly, the beneficiary must become beneficially entitled to an interest in possession on the deceased’s death, the settlement must be effected by the deceased’s will or intestacy, and the interest must not fall within the excluded special categories specified by the section. The exact current wording and connected provisions must be applied.

A qualifying interest is estate-linked for IHT under section 49. This does not mean the beneficiary owns the trust capital for trust-law purposes, nor does it decide the trustees’ Income Tax or CGT treatment. Powers to terminate or appoint property and later variations must be analysed separately.

Illustrative example: Daniel’s spouse, Mei, is immediately entitled under his will to occupy the home and receive net income for life. The trustees test section 49A from Daniel’s death and the precise will terms. Calling Mei a “life tenant” is a useful description but is not the statutory test.

Younger beneficiaries

A trust for a bereaved minor under IHTA 1984 section 71A has detailed conditions, including its permitted source and the minor becoming absolutely entitled no later than age 18. An age 18-to-25 trust under section 71D has a different set of conditions and permits entitlement at an age not exceeding 25. Neither treatment follows simply because a beneficiary is young.

If the statutory conditions are not met, the property may instead be relevant property and subject to the ordinary anniversary and exit-charge framework. The will should therefore specify vesting age, income and advancement provisions carefully, and the file should record the deceased’s relationship to the beneficiary and any later variation or appointment.

Illustrative example: Daniel leaves one fund to his 15-year-old daughter absolutely at 18 and another to his 17-year-old nephew at 25. The first may meet the bereaved-minor conditions because Daniel was her parent; the nephew’s fund does not qualify merely because it uses age 25 wording. Each fund requires its own statutory classification.

Practical issues and safeguards

Do not assume that every provision in a will is automatically outside the relevant-property regime or exempt from TRS. Statutory definitions, the two-year will-trust exclusion and later variations require separate tests.

  • Separate estate administration from the later trust and record the capacity change.
  • Classify each will interest for IHT and treat later variations as new events.
  • Test TRS exclusions, expiry and taxable-trust duties independently.

With those safeguards recorded, the illustration below brings the four findings together and tests whether the documents, chronology and proposed treatment remain consistent.

Illustration

Fictional estate and family trusts: Daniel dies on 12 June 2026. His will gives Mei an immediate right to occupy the family home and receive its net income for life, with capital then to his children. Residue is held for his daughter Lila at 18 and his nephew Owen at 25 in separate shares. The same two people are executors and trustees.

The estate includes the home, investments and a business. Liabilities are unresolved for 14 months. The home is appropriated to its trust after six months; residue is ascertained after 18 months; and the trusts remain in existence two years after death.

How the file should be resolved

  1. Prepare one chronology from death through probate, asset sales, appropriation, ascertainment of residue and trust commencement.
  2. Separate personal-representative and trustee capacities, bank activity and accounts.
  3. Map every specific legacy, residuary share, income right, occupation right and default gift.
  4. Test Mei’s interest under sections 49 and 49A using the will terms and date of death.
  5. Test Lila and Owen separately under sections 71A and 71D; do not infer classification from age alone.
  6. Reconcile estate Income Tax and CGT with the trusts’ opening assets and later returns.
  7. Diarise TRS, IHT, Self Assessment and beneficiary-information obligations using the actual commencement and exclusion rules.

Applied analysis: The executors and trustees are the same people but not acting in the same capacity throughout. Mei’s home trust, the residuary trust and the two younger-beneficiary shares may have different commencement and tax classifications. The two-year TRS exclusion is a temporary registration rule, not proof that the ongoing trusts are otherwise exempt.
