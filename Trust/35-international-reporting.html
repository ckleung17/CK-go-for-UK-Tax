Operational framework

Study checkpoint: requirements current on 17 July 2026. A trust may be a Financial Institution, a trustee-documented trust or a non-financial entity for the Common Reporting Standard (CRS) and FATCA. Classification drives registration, due diligence, self-certification and reporting. It is separate from UK tax residence and TRS classification.

Entity classification may turn on whether the trust is a managed investment entity, uses a trustee-documented trust, or is a passive NFE; that classification then determines the reportable interests.

The detailed analysis begins with Managed Investment Entity, which provides the foundation for the concepts that follow.

Classifying a trust for international reporting

This topic turns on four linked questions. Resolve them in sequence using the governing instrument, event chronology and supporting evidence, because the answer to one question can alter the next.

Managed Investment Entity

A trust whose financial assets are professionally managed can fall within the Investment Entity definition, subject to income and activity tests.

That conclusion leads to the next question: trustee-documented trust.

Trustee-documented trust

Where conditions are met, a reporting Financial Institution trustee undertakes reporting, but the trust has its own mandatory registration considerations.

That conclusion leads to the next question: passive nfe.

Passive NFE

If not a Financial Institution, a passive entity may need to disclose controlling persons to the institution maintaining its financial account.

That conclusion leads to the next question: reportable interests.

Reportable interests

Settlors, beneficiaries, trustees or other persons can be account holders or controlling persons depending on classification and regime.

Taken together, these concepts determine which records, checks and safeguards are needed in practice.

Practical issues and safeguards

TRS registration does not satisfy CRS or FATCA. The regimes use different definitions of residence, beneficial owner, controlling person and reportable account, and 2025–26 regulatory changes introduced new registration and data requirements.

  • Classify the entity independently under CRS and FATCA using current definitions.
  • Identify residence, controlling persons, reportable accounts and trustee-documented responsibilities.
  • Do not reuse TRS data without checking different definitions and mandatory fields.
