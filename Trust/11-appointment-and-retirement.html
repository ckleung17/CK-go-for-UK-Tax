Appointment and retirement of trustees

Trust administration — changing the legal owners

HomeTrust Index

Overview

Changing trustees changes the people or corporation responsible for the office and legal title; it does not ordinarily create a new trust. For an England-and-Wales trust, authority may come from the instrument, the Trustee Act 1925, the Trusts of Land and Appointment of Trustees Act 1996 or the court. Other jurisdictions have different rules.

Complete four connected checks:

  1. Who has authority to appoint or discharge? Identify the operative deed provision or statutory appointor and every required consent.
  2. Will the continuing trustee body be valid? Check number, capacity, eligibility, conflicts and any asset-specific requirement.
  3. How will every legal title and mandate be changed? Treat execution, statutory vesting and third-party registration as separate steps.
  4. Will the appointment alter tax residence or reporting? Apply the current residence rules before fixing the effective time.

Authority comes first because an invalid appointor cannot create a valid change. The proposed composition then determines whether retirement can take effect; only then can title, tax residence and reporting be completed coherently.

Changing the trustee body safely

Appointor and authority

Read the latest consolidated trust terms, including every variation and earlier appointment. An express power may name the settlor, a protector or another person as appointor. If it does not provide an effective route, section 36 Trustee Act 1925 may permit the person nominated for the purpose or, in default, the surviving or continuing trustees to appoint a replacement in specified circumstances.

Record why the statutory condition is satisfied, whether the outgoing trustee has capacity, and whether consent, notice or court involvement is required. Holding a power of appointment does not itself make the appointor a trustee. A power must be exercised for its proper purpose and in the form required by the instrument and law.

Illustrative example: A deed names the settlor as appointor during life, but the settlor has died. The remaining trustees cannot simply ask the principal beneficiary to sign. They establish whether the deed provides a successor and, if not, whether section 36 transfers the appointment function to them.

Minimum composition and discharge

A replacement appointment and retirement without replacement use different machinery. Section 39 Trustee Act 1925 permits retirement by deed where its conditions are met, including consent and the continuation of at least two individuals or a trust corporation. The instrument or a particular transaction may impose stricter requirements.

Check age, mental capacity, corporate status, insolvency, conflicts, willingness and practical suitability. Land transactions and receipt of capital money may require the appropriate trustee structure. The deed should identify the precise effective time, the continuing and incoming trustees, discharge and any indemnity without purporting to excuse an unreviewed breach.

Illustrative example: One of two individual trustees wants to retire without replacement. Section 39 is not satisfied merely because the sole remaining trustee agrees. The trust appoints a suitable replacement or uses another valid route before the retirement can be completed.

Vesting and implementation

Section 40 can allow a deed appointing or discharging trustees to operate as a vesting declaration for qualifying property, but statutory exceptions and the asset’s own transfer system must be checked. Registered land, company shares, investment accounts, bank mandates, intellectual property, partnerships and foreign assets may each need distinct forms, consents or registrations.

Prepare an asset-by-asset completion schedule. Reconcile custody, signing authority, online access, insurance, contracts and professional engagements. The outgoing trustee should not be treated as fully removed from operational records until completion evidence shows that no asset or authority remains stranded in the former name.

Illustrative example: A retirement deed includes a vesting declaration, but the fund owns registered land, private-company shares and a French account. The trustees separately complete Land Registry, company-register and French-bank requirements and retain confirmations against the asset schedule.

Tax residence and reporting

For Income Tax and CGT, trustees are treated as a single person and residence depends on the trustee composition and, for a mixed body, statutory facts concerning the settlor. HMRC’s current guidance also distinguishes settlements affected by the changes from 6 April 2025. The analysis must use the settlement’s creation and addition history, not simply the incoming trustee’s address.

A migration can have tax and reporting consequences beyond the appointment itself. Even where residence does not change, Trust Registration Service details, Self Assessment records, financial institutions, Common Reporting Standard and Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act classifications and responsible contacts may need updating. Registrable trust changes generally need to be reflected within the applicable 90-day period.

Illustrative example: Two UK-resident trustees propose appointing a Jersey corporate trustee and retiring together at midnight. Before signing, advisers model the residence position at each instant, examine the settlor and addition history, and coordinate tax, TRS, banking and international-reporting steps so there is no unintended intermediate composition.

If a trustee dies

The death of an individual trustee does not ordinarily end the trust or make the trust fund part of that trustee’s beneficial estate. Any surviving trustees normally continue to administer the trust, subject to the deed, the nature of the assets and rules requiring a minimum number or particular type of trustee for some transactions.

Start with the trust instrument: it may name the person entitled to appoint a replacement. If it does not provide an effective route, section 36 Trustee Act 1925 may permit the statutory appointor, the surviving or continuing trustees, or in specified circumstances the personal representatives of the last surviving or continuing trustee to appoint a new trustee. A court appointment may be required if the available machinery cannot operate or there is a dispute.

Appointment is only part of the process. Complete every required vesting, Land Registry, bank, investment-provider and corporate-register step; review control of records and digital access; notify insurers and advisers; and update the Trust Registration Service where required. Recheck trustee residence before appointment because changing the trustee body can have tax consequences. Do not make substantive decisions until authority, title and any minimum-composition rule have been confirmed.

Practical issues and safeguards

Never assume signing a retirement deed alone removes all liability or title. Confirm indemnities, outstanding breaches, tax filings and whether third-party registers have actually been changed.

  • Identify the appointment or retirement power, appointor and required consents before drafting.
  • Check trustee number, capacity, residence, conflicts and suitability before execution.
  • Complete vesting, register, banking, TRS and signing-authority changes and retain completion evidence.

With those safeguards recorded, the illustration below brings the four findings together and tests whether the documents, chronology and proposed treatment remain consistent.

Illustration

Fictional trustee transition: A 2012 England-and-Wales discretionary trust has three individual trustees: Grace and Leo are UK resident; Marta has moved permanently to Spain and wants to retire. The deceased settlor was the named appointor. The fund owns English registered land, quoted investments, shares in a private company and a Spanish bank account.

The trustees propose appointing Atlas Trust Co, a Jersey company, and allowing Marta to retire on the same day. A protector must consent under a 2019 variation. The family assumes the appointment deed will transfer everything automatically and will not affect residence.

How the transition should proceed

  1. Assemble the original deed, variations and complete trustee-history schedule.
  2. Confirm the appointor, statutory route, protector consent and Atlas’s suitability.
  3. Model the trustee composition and tax residence at every proposed effective time.
  4. Draft appointment, retirement, vesting, discharge and indemnity provisions against that analysis.
  5. Prepare a completion schedule for each asset, mandate, contract, record system and jurisdiction.
  6. Execute in the intended order and verify every registration and third-party acknowledgement.
  7. Update TRS, tax, regulatory and reporting records and retain a signed closing reconciliation.

Applied analysis: The deceased appointor, protector consent, Jersey company and foreign account prevent this from being a simple signature exercise. Marta should not be treated as operationally discharged, nor Atlas as holding every asset, until the authority, timing, residence and completion evidence agree.
