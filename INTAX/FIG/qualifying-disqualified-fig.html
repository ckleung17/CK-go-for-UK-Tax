Qualifying and Disqualified Foreign Income and Gains

A practical map of the amounts that can, and cannot, be included in a four-year FIG claim.

Start with the Statutory Category

Scope: This page concerns the UK four-year FIG regime for 2025/26 onwards and reflects enacted law and official guidance checked on 17 July 2026.

Qualifying-new-resident status does not make every overseas receipt tax-free. The individual must first calculate the amount under the ordinary rules for that source, decide whether it is qualifying foreign income or a qualifying foreign gain, and identify it in a valid annual claim. UK-source amounts and statutory disqualified income remain taxable.

Qualifying Foreign Income

Section 845H ITTOIA 2005 contains a defined list. Common categories include profits of a trade carried on wholly outside the UK, a partner's share of such profits, overseas-property profits, foreign interest and dividends, royalties, estate income, foreign pensions and social-security benefits, offshore income gains and specified income attributed under the settlements or Transfer of Assets Abroad rules.

Foreign employment income is not claimed under the ordinary foreign-income claim. Qualifying employment income relating to overseas duties is considered separately under Overseas Workday Relief.

Disqualified Income

Section 845I excludes specified amounts even if they have a foreign connection. The exclusions include certain settlement income originating before 6 April 2025, income from a security treated as UK-situated following a qualifying share exchange involving a non-UK close company, transferred income streams, performance income of entertainers and sportspersons, and specified pension payments.

The label attached to an account or entity does not decide the result. For example, a disposal of an interest in a non-reporting offshore fund can produce an offshore income gain taxed as income rather than a capital gain. Its character must be established before selecting a return page or FIG claim.

Qualifying Foreign Gains

A straightforward qualifying foreign asset gain is a chargeable gain on an asset situated outside the UK that does not derive at least 75% of its value from UK land. Pre-6 April 2025 remittance-basis gains do not become qualifying merely because they are realised or remitted during the four-year period.

Relief can also cover specified gains attributed from a non-UK close company, qualifying asset holding company gains, gains attributed to settlors of non-resident settlements and gains matched to capital payments received by beneficiaries. Each category has its own identification and pooling consequences.

Illustration

Priya is a qualifying new resident. During 2026/27 she receives foreign bank interest, earns income from overseas employment duties and disposes of an interest in a non-reporting offshore fund.

  • The foreign interest is tested under the foreign-income claim.
  • The employment income is tested under OWR, not included automatically with the interest.
  • The offshore-fund disposal must be classified before deciding whether it produces income or a gain.
  • Each eligible amount is calculated and claimed separately.

Applied analysis: Source, statutory character and claim route come before the decision whether claiming relief is worthwhile.

Practical Checklist

  • Calculate each source under its ordinary tax rules.
  • Separate UK and foreign components.
  • Check the statutory qualifying and disqualified lists.
  • Identify attributed income, gains and trust payments separately.
  • Keep evidence of source, situs, acquisition, disposal and foreign tax.
