Overseas Employers: PAYE and NIC Responsibilities

OWR applies to a qualifying UK-resident employee whose employment income relates to duties performed wholly or partly outside the UK. It determines the employee's Income Tax relief; it does not by itself determine who must operate PAYE or whether UK National Insurance contributions (NIC) are payable.

1. Overseas employer with a UK presence

If the overseas employer has a UK branch, agency, representative office or other UK tax presence, it will normally register with HMRC and operate PAYE. It must also account for NIC where the international social-security rules make UK NIC applicable.

2. Employee works for a UK host business

If the overseas employer has no UK presence but the employee works for a UK host, section 689 ITEPA 2003 may require the UK host to operate PAYE on the overseas earnings, often through a shadow payroll. Separate NIC rules may also treat the UK host as responsible for employee and employer NIC.

3. No UK presence and no UK host

If neither exists, the overseas employer may operate PAYE voluntarily. Otherwise, HMRC may agree a PAYE Direct Payment arrangement under which the employee accounts for PAYE on their own earnings, or require the Income Tax position to be settled through Self Assessment. If UK NIC applies and no UK person is treated as the employer, the employee may also need a direct-payment arrangement for employee Class 1 NIC.

NIC may be excluded

UK NIC may not apply where the employee has an A1 or other certificate of coverage, a reciprocal social-security agreement assigns coverage to another country, or the conditions for a temporary-posting exemption are satisfied.
