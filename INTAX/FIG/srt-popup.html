The SRT Framework Overview

The Statutory Residence Test (SRT) determines an individual's UK tax residency status independently for each tax year. Taxpayers must evaluate their circumstances by progressing through three distinct steps in a strict priority order. As soon as a criterion in a step is satisfied, the residence status is definitively settled, and you do not proceed further.

Step 1: Automatic Overseas Tests

If an individual meets any of these conditions, they are conclusively **non-UK tax resident** for the year:

  • Spends fewer than 16 days in the UK during the tax year (and was a UK resident in one or more of the three preceding tax years).
  • Spends fewer than 46 days in the UK during the tax year (and was non-resident in all three preceding tax years).
  • Works full-time overseas (averaging at least 35 hours per week), spending fewer than 91 days in the UK, and working in the UK for more than 3 hours on fewer than 31 days.

Step 2: Automatic UK Tests

If no overseas tests are satisfied, an individual is deemed an **automatic UK tax resident** if they meet any of the following:

  • Spends 183 days or more physically present in the UK during the tax year.
  • Has a home in the UK available for a continuous period of at least 91 days (with at least 30 days falling in the tax year), uses that home for at least 30 days, and either has no overseas home or spends fewer than 30 days in their overseas home.
  • Works full-time in the UK for a rolling 365-day period, where more than 75% of those total workdays are performed within the UK.

Step 3: Sufficient Ties Test

If an individual's status remains unresolved by Steps 1 and 2, their residency is determined by balancing the exact number of physical days spent in the UK against their connecting ties. The fewer days an individual spends in the UK, the more ties they can maintain before triggering tax residence. The five core ties evaluated by HMRC are:

  1. Family Tie: Having a UK tax-resident spouse, civil partner, cohabiting partner, or minor child.
  2. Accommodation Tie: Having a place of residence available in the UK for a continuous block of at least 91 days and spending at least one night there (or 16 nights if it is a close relative's home).
  3. Work Tie: Working in the UK for 40 days or more in a tax year (where a "day of work" is defined as performing more than 3 hours of duties).
  4. 90-Day Tie: Spending more than 90 days physically present in the UK in either of the previous two tax years.
  5. Country Tie (Leavers Only): Spending more midnights in the UK than in any other single country during the tax year.
