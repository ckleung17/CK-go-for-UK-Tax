Tax treatment

Study checkpoint: 2026/27. Trust income cannot be assigned to a taxpayer merely by looking at who receives cash. Start with beneficial entitlement and the trust type, then apply trustee charging provisions and settlements legislation. The same income may be paid by trustees but treated as the settlor’s or beneficiary’s for final liability.

Charging responsibility depends on whether the arrangement is a bare trust, an interest in possession, an accumulation or discretionary trust, or a settlor-interested settlement.

Identifying the person charged to Income Tax

Bare trust

The beneficiary is normally taxable on income arising from the underlying assets, whether or not cash is withdrawn.

Analyse the substance rather than the label. Read the trust instrument with the applicable legislation and current official guidance, identify the person whose rights or obligations are affected, and distinguish the trust-law answer from each tax and reporting question. The file should state the effective date, asset or amount involved and evidence supporting the conclusion. Once that position is recorded, it can be carried into the analysis of interest in possession without assuming that the same legal or tax classification applies.

Practical issues and safeguards

Trust labels are not enough. A mixed trust, partial settlor interest, mandate or change during the year can divide the income between different charging regimes.

  • Divide the trust into funds and periods where rights or settlor interests differ.
  • Identify the legal recipient and statutory taxpayer for every income source.
  • Reconcile trustee returns, beneficiary statements and settlor reporting without duplication.

With those safeguards recorded, the illustration below brings the relevant findings together and tests whether the documents, chronology and proposed treatment remain consistent.

Illustration

A trust owns shares and rental property. One fund gives Alex all net income; another lets trustees choose among grandchildren; the settlor can benefit from a third. Each fund requires a separate taxpayer and reporting analysis even if one bank account receives everything.

Applied analysis: Separate the three funds and income sources, apply the relevant charging regime to each, allocate expenses and produce matching trustee and recipient records.
