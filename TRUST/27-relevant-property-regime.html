IHT framework

Study checkpoint: chargeable events in 2026/27. Relevant-property trusts are subject to a principal charge at each ten-year anniversary and proportionate charges when property leaves the regime or value is reduced by a disposition. The headline maximum of 6% is not a flat charge: the effective rate depends on the statutory history and calculation.

Determine the relevant property fund before calculating any principal charge, proportionate charge, or charge connected with accumulated income.

Following the relevant-property charging cycle

Relevant property

Broadly, settled property not within excluded property, a qualifying interest in possession or a statutory special-trust category.

Analyse the substance rather than the label. Read the trust instrument with the applicable legislation and current official guidance, identify the person whose rights or obligations are affected, and distinguish the trust-law answer from each tax and reporting question. The file should state the effective date, asset or amount involved and evidence supporting the conclusion. Once that position is recorded, it can be carried into the analysis of principal charge without assuming that the same legal or tax classification applies.

Practical issues and safeguards

Separate deeds are not always separate for the calculation. Related settlements, multiple settlements created on the same day and additions can affect the available nil-rate band and effective rate.

  • Maintain a full settlement history from commencement through the charge date.
  • Obtain defensible values and identify excluded, relieved and accumulated property.
  • Compute the statutory effective rate before applying it to anniversary or exit property.

Illustration

A discretionary trust reaches its first ten-year anniversary with investments, property and six-year-old undistributed income. Trustees cannot apply 6% to the balance. They must value net relevant property, test reliefs, include statutory historical amounts and calculate the effective rate before any exit computation.
