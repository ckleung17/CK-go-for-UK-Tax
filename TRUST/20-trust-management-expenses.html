Tax treatment

Study checkpoint: 2026/27. Trust-management expenses are costs of administering the trust as a whole rather than expenses incurred to earn a particular income source. Their trust-law allocation between income and capital and their Income Tax effect are separate questions. Detailed rules affect the measure of beneficiary income and special-rate income.

Distinguish deductible source expenses from trust expenditure that is income or capital, then apply the ordering rules separately for an IIP beneficiary and a discretionary trust.

Classifying and allocating trust expenses

Source expenses

Expenses wholly and exclusively incurred for a rental business or trade are considered in that source computation, not again as trust-management expenses.

Analyse the substance rather than the label. Read the trust instrument with the applicable legislation and current official guidance, identify the person whose rights or obligations are affected, and distinguish the trust-law answer from each tax and reporting question. The file should state the effective date, asset or amount involved and evidence supporting the conclusion. Once that position is recorded, it can be carried into the analysis of income or capital without assuming that the same legal or tax classification applies.

Practical issues and safeguards

The accounting decision to debit income does not guarantee tax deductibility. Mixed invoices should be supported by a reasonable, evidenced allocation and reviewed under the rules for the relevant trust type.

  • Analyse each invoice by service, beneficiary purpose and accounting period.
  • Separate source deductions, trust-management expenses and capital costs.
  • Document reasonable allocation for mixed bills and apply the correct ordering rules.
