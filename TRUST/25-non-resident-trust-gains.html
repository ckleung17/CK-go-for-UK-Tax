Tax treatment

Study checkpoint: 2026/27. Non-UK resident trustees may be directly charged on specified UK assets, while trust gains can also be attributed to a UK-resident settlor or matched to capital payments received by UK-resident beneficiaries. The post-6 April 2025 FIG rules add claims and pool adjustments rather than replacing sections 86 and 87.

Analyse the direct trustee charge, possible settlor attribution, later beneficiary matching, and any FIG interaction in sequence rather than treating an offshore trust as a single charge.

Tracing non-resident trust gains

Direct trustee charge

Non-resident trustees can be within UK CGT for direct or indirect disposals of UK land and other statutory cases.

Practical issues and safeguards

Bank transfers alone do not reveal the tax result. Loans, asset use, third-party payments and onward gifts can be capital payments or benefits. Historical pre-2025 pools must not be merged blindly with later gains.

  • Maintain separate historic gain pools by relevant period and sterling amount.
  • Classify cash, loans, asset use, expenses and onward transfers as possible benefits or capital payments.
  • Test settlor attribution, beneficiary matching and FIG consequences in the correct order.
