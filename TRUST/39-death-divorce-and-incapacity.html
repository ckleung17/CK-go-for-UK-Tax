Integrated framework

Study checkpoint: law and guidance checked to 17 July 2026. Death, relationship breakdown and incapacity can alter who controls property, who benefits and which court or statutory rules apply. Trustees must distinguish estate administration, continuing trusts, matrimonial or family orders, powers of attorney and special provision for vulnerable beneficiaries.

The file should distinguish death, divorce or dissolution, incapacity, and any variation after death, because each event changes authority and beneficial rights in a different way.

Separating the effects of major life events

Death

Identify assets in the free estate, jointly owned property, qualifying trust interests, death benefits and property already held by trustees.

Analyse the substance rather than the label. Read the trust instrument with the applicable legislation and current official guidance, identify the person whose rights or obligations are affected, and distinguish the trust-law answer from each tax and reporting question. The file should state the effective date, asset or amount involved and evidence supporting the conclusion. Once that position is recorded, it can be carried into the analysis of divorce or dissolution without assuming that the same legal or tax classification applies.

Whose death or incapacity?

Trustee: the trust normally continues. Confirm who can act, appoint a replacement where necessary, complete title and registration changes, and distinguish the deceased trustee’s personal representatives from the continuing trustee body.

Settlor: a lifetime trust does not normally end merely because the settlor dies. Death can nevertheless alter attribution rules, foreign-property treatment, reservation-of-benefit analysis, reporting and the information recorded about the settlor.

Beneficiary: the result depends on the interest. Death may end a life interest, accelerate a remainder, leave a vested interest in the beneficiary’s estate, or simply remove one object from a discretionary class. Each outcome may create different Income Tax, CGT and IHT consequences.

Person with several roles: analyse each capacity separately. Someone may be a trustee, beneficiary and personal representative at the same time, but must identify which authority supports each act, manage conflicts and keep separate records. Loss of capacity also requires a role-by-role review; an attorney does not automatically become a trustee.

Practical issues and safeguards

Do not let an executor, attorney, beneficiary or family member act as trustee unless validly appointed or otherwise authorised. Conflicts and privilege require particular care in family disputes.

  • Identify each person’s capacity and legal authority after the event.
  • Separate trustee replacement, estate administration, beneficial succession and family-court issues.
  • Secure records, manage conflicts and obtain court or specialist directions where authority is disputed.

A life tenant dies while divorce proceedings involving a remainder beneficiary are ongoing, and another trustee lacks capacity. Applied analysis: Process the life-interest termination, trustee incapacity and divorce dispute as separate files, appoint an authorised decision-maker and coordinate reporting without assuming one role confers another.
