Tax treatment

Study checkpoint: 2026/27. For 2026/27, UK-resident trustees are generally charged on trust gains after allowable losses and the trust annual exempt amount. Trustees are treated as a single continuing person for CGT, even when individuals change. Residence, the asset, the event and any beneficiary entitlement must be fixed before computing the gain.

The computation begins with trustee residence and the trustees as a single continuing body, before applying the correct annual exempt amount and rate for the year.

Establishing trustee CGT liability

Apply the statutory trustee-residence rules for the tax year; governing law and asset location do not decide residence.

Annual exempt amount

For 2026/27 the ordinary trust annual exempt amount is £1,500, potentially divided among qualifying related settlements; vulnerable-beneficiary trusts can have a different amount.

Practical issues and safeguards

Do not use an individual beneficiary’s annual exempt amount or personal rate for a trustee gain. A bare trust or an occasion of absolute entitlement may instead place the gain in a different taxpayer or at a different time.

  • Confirm trustee residence, beneficial ownership and the disposal date before computing.
  • Reconstruct base cost, enhancement expenditure, losses and related-settlement AEA division.
  • Test relief, attribution and absolute entitlement before applying trustee rates.
