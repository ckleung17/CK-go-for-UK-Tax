IHT framework

Study checkpoint: chargeable events in 2026/27. IHT treatment depends on when an interest in possession arose and whether it is a qualifying interest. Pre-22 March 2006 interests and specified later interests can cause the life tenant to be treated as beneficially entitled to the underlying property; other post-2006 interests are commonly within relevant property.

An interest-in-possession review distinguishes a pre-22 March 2006 interest, an immediate post-death interest, a disabled person’s interest, and a transitional serial interest, because their Inheritance Tax treatment can differ.

Classifying interests in possession for IHT

Pre-22 March 2006 interest

Generally remains within section 49 estate-linked treatment, subject to transitional changes and later events.

Practical issues and safeguards

A trust can contain mixed funds with different IHT treatment, especially after additions. Do not allow the oldest interest or the deed’s general label to determine every asset.

  • Split mixed funds by creation date, source and statutory category.
  • Identify whose estate includes each qualifying interest and when it terminates.
  • Review additions, appointments and serial interests as possible reclassification events.

With those safeguards recorded, the illustration below brings the relevant findings together and tests whether the documents, chronology and proposed treatment remain consistent.

Illustration

A spouse receives a life interest under a will following a death in 2026. If it is an immediate post-death interest, the trust property is generally treated as part of the spouse’s estate for IHT. A similar lifetime-created interest in 2026 would not qualify merely because it pays all income.
