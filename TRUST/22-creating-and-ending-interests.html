Tax treatment

Study checkpoint: 2026/27. Trust CGT events do not depend only on a sale. A settlor can dispose of an asset when it enters trust, trustees can make actual or deemed disposals, and section 71 TCGA 1992 can deem a market-value disposal when a beneficiary becomes absolutely entitled as against them.

A transfer into trust, later absolute entitlement, a later transfer, and termination on death are separate disposal points whose timing and consideration must be established.

Recognising CGT disposal points

Transfer into trust

A gift normally uses market value between connected or non-arm’s-length parties, subject to any valid hold-over relief.

Analyse the substance rather than the label. Read the trust instrument with the applicable legislation and current official guidance, identify the person whose rights or obligations are affected, and distinguish the trust-law answer from each tax and reporting question. The file should state the effective date, asset or amount involved and evidence supporting the conclusion. Once that position is recorded, it can be carried into the analysis of absolute entitlement without assuming that the same legal or tax classification applies.

Absolute entitlement

When the beneficiary obtains exclusive control for CGT purposes, trustees are deemed to dispose of and reacquire the asset at market value as bare trustees.

Practical issues and safeguards

Absolute entitlement can arise before paperwork or payment. Conversely, a vested share in a mixed fund may not create entitlement to a specific asset until appropriation. Analyse the rights asset by asset.

  • Read the entitlement provisions asset by asset and identify the exact vesting event.
  • Obtain event-date market values and trustee base costs before later paperwork obscures timing.
  • Check appropriation, hold-over claims and reporting for both trustees and beneficiary.

With those safeguards recorded, the illustration below brings the relevant findings together and tests whether the documents, chronology and proposed treatment remain consistent.

Illustration

A beneficiary reaches 30 and becomes absolutely entitled to a portfolio worth £400,000, while trustees’ base cost is £250,000. Section 71 may create a £150,000 trustee gain at that date even though the portfolio remains registered to trustees for several months.

Applied analysis: Treat the thirtieth birthday as the potential disposal date, value the portfolio then, compute the trustee gain and reconcile later registration as completion rather than timing.
