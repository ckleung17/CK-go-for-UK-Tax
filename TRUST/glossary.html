Glossary of Terms

Select a term from any Trust topic page to open its definition here in a popup window. The glossary is arranged alphabetically.

A

Absolute entitlement

In Bare trusts and nominees: The beneficiary?s rights are fixed; the trustee has no dispositive discretion over who benefits.

In Creating and ending interests: When the beneficiary obtains exclusive control for CGT purposes, trustees are deemed to dispose of and reacquire the asset at market value as bare trustees.

See: Bare trusts and nominees; Creating and ending interests

Account of profits

An unauthorised fiduciary profit may be recoverable even where the trust cannot show conventional loss.

See: Breach of trust and remedies

Accumulated income

Undistributed income held for more than five years can be treated as relevant property at an anniversary under specific rules.

See: Relevant-property regime

Accumulation

Income retained under a power may later become capital for trust-law purposes; accounts must preserve its origin and tax history.

See: Discretionary and accumulation trusts

Accumulation or discretionary trust

Trustees are charged under the special trust-rate framework; later income distributions use the tax-pool rules.

See: Who is taxable

Administration period

Personal representatives collect assets, settle liabilities and establish the residue before trust property is fully ascertained.

See: Will and family trusts

Age 18-to-25 trust

A qualifying will or intestacy trust can postpone absolute entitlement beyond 18 but applies a modified charge when property leaves before 25.

See: Special trusts and reliefs

Annual exempt amount

For 2026/27 the ordinary trust annual exempt amount is £1,500, potentially divided among qualifying related settlements; vulnerable-beneficiary trusts can have a different amount.

See: Trustee chargeability

Annual file

Accounts, tax returns, valuations, investment reviews, distributions, minutes and compliance calendars document each period.

See: Governance and evidence

Appointor

The deed may name a person with power to appoint replacements; otherwise statutory powers may pass to continuing trustees or other persons.

See: Appointment and retirement of trustees

Asset reliefs

Agricultural property relief, business relief and charity exemption depend on current conditions, ownership periods, asset use and post-6 April 2026 limits.

See: Special trusts and reliefs

Available nil-rate band

Chargeable transfers in the preceding seven years reduce the band available to the current transfer.

See: Transfers into trust

B

Bare trust

The beneficiary is normally taxable on income arising from the underlying assets, whether or not cash is withdrawn.

See: Who is taxable

Beneficial interest

A beneficiary may have a fixed entitlement, an interest in income or capital, or only a right to proper administration of a discretion.

See: Nature of a trust

Beneficial owners

TRS records settlors, trustees, named or class beneficiaries and persons exercising control, with additional tax data for taxable trusts.

See: Trust Registration Service

Beneficiary

A person, class of people or permitted body entitled to benefit, or eligible to be considered for benefit, under the trust terms. The interest may relate to income, capital or both and may be fixed or discretionary.

See: Nature of a trust

Beneficiary matching

Section 87 can match trust gains with capital payments to beneficiaries; unmatched gains and payments may carry forward.

See: Non-resident trust gains

Beneficiary termination

Under the Saunders v Vautier principle, all beneficiaries must generally be adult, capable, ascertained and together absolutely entitled.

See: Variation and termination

Bereaved-minor trust

Generally derives from a deceased parent’s will, intestacy or specified compensation arrangement and requires absolute entitlement by 18.

See: Special trusts and reliefs

Business reliefs

Business Asset Disposal Relief and gift relief require specific beneficiary and business connections; trust ownership alone is insufficient.

See: Private residence and business reliefs

C

Capacity and authority

Check the settlor’s capacity, corporate authority, ownership of the asset and any restrictions on disposition.

See: Creation and validity

Capital Gains Tax

TCGA rules generally look through trustees where the beneficiary is absolutely entitled, subject to specific asset and statutory rules.

See: Bare trusts and nominees

Capital payment

Not automatically Income Tax-free: non-resident trust matching, settlor rules or anti-avoidance provisions may apply.

See: Beneficiary receipts

Capital transactions

Sales, incorporations, appointments and reorganisations can trigger CGT, SDLT and valuation rules even without external cash.

See: Trust-owned businesses and property

Cessation

Giving up a reserved benefit can be treated as a potentially exempt transfer at cessation, creating a new seven-year period.

See: Settlor benefits and reservations

Charitable trust

Must satisfy charity-law requirements and tax conditions; charity registration and HMRC recognition are separate processes.

See: Commercial and specialist trusts

Co-ownership or security trust

May hold title for owners, lenders or investors with rights principally defined by property or commercial documents.

See: Commercial and specialist trusts

Constitution

A promise to transfer property is not the same as a completed transfer. Identify how title to each asset passes and whether anything remains to be done.

See: Creation and validity

Constructive trust

Imposed by law in circumstances where the legal owner cannot in good conscience assert the whole beneficial interest. Domestic-property claims are highly fact-sensitive.

See: Types of trust and how they arise

Court variation

The Variation of Trusts Act 1958 permits court approval on behalf of specified persons where the statutory benefit test is met.

See: Variation and termination

D

Data governance

Trustees handle sensitive personal and financial data and need controlled access, retention, lawful processing and secure disposal.

See: Governance and evidence

De minimis amount

HMRC guidance describes a normally £500 trust-income amount, divided across certain multiple settlements; if income exceeds the applicable amount, the exemption is lost rather than applied as an allowance.

See: Trust income

Deadline

For relevant post-6 April 2014 trust events, reporting and payment are generally due by the end of the sixth month after the event.

See: IHT reporting

Deadlines

Paper returns are generally due 31 October and electronic returns 31 January following the tax year; trust online filing normally requires commercial software.

See: Trust Self Assessment

Death

Identify assets in the free estate, jointly owned property, qualifying trust interests, death benefits and property already held by trustees.

See: Death, divorce and incapacity

Death within seven years

The transfer is recalculated at death rates, with credit for lifetime tax and possible taper relief on tax, not on the transferred value.

See: Transfers into trust

Deceased settlor

If death occurred from 6 April 2025, status generally follows long-term residence immediately before death; pre-6 April 2025 deaths retain a domicile-based test for settled property.

See: Foreign property from 6 April 2025

Deferred amount

The held-over gain normally reduces the recipient’s acquisition cost; it is not erased.

See: Hold-over relief

Delegation

Permitted functions, written policy, agent selection and continuing review must be distinguished from an abdication of responsibility.

See: Trustee duties and powers

Delivery threshold

Some events need not be delivered where statutory or administrative conditions are met, but records must support the conclusion.

See: IHT reporting

Direct trustee charge

Non-resident trustees can be within UK CGT for direct or indirect disposals of UK land and other statutory cases.

See: Non-resident trust gains

Disabled person’s interest

Specified trusts can deem the disabled person beneficially entitled for IHT.

See: IHT and interests in possession

Disabled-person trust

Special treatment depends on the statutory definition of disabled person and strict limits on benefit for others.

See: Special trusts and reliefs

Disclosure and GAAR

DOTAS, DAC6 where applicable, CRS/FATCA and GAAR processes are distinct; disclosure does not determine whether tax is due.

See: Anti-avoidance and disclosure

Discretionary class

An object has a right to proper administration and genuine consideration, but no fixed share before an appointment.

See: Discretionary and accumulation trusts

Discretionary eligibility

A settlor included in a discretionary class can create a reservation even if trustees never make an actual payment.

See: Settlor benefits and reservations

Discretionary income payment

For a UK-resident non-settlor-interested trust, the payment carries a trust-rate credit and is grossed for the beneficiary’s return.

See: Beneficiary receipts

Discretionary object

Has a right to due administration and consideration, but no fixed entitlement to a payment until trustees exercise their discretion.

See: Beneficial interests

Discretionary trust

Qualifying expenses can affect the amount charged at special rates and require grossing at the corresponding ordinary rate.

See: Trust management expenses

Divorce or dissolution

Trust interests can be resources, property or targets of court orders; tax consequences depend on the actual order and implementation date.

See: Death, divorce and incapacity

Document control

Maintain executed deeds, wills, appointments, tax returns, contracts, advice and beneficiary communications securely.

See: Decision-making and records

Document map

List every deed, will, appointment, loan, account, valuation and tax return, with execution and effective dates.

See: Case analysis and worked examples

Duty of care

Trustee Act 2000 applies a statutory standard to specified functions, adjusted for a trustee’s knowledge, experience and professional status.

See: Trustee duties and powers

E

Employee benefit trust

Usually holds assets for employees or former employees; employment-income, Part 7A, corporation-tax and IHT rules may overlap.

See: Commercial and specialist trusts

Entitlement to occupy

The beneficiary must occupy under the settlement terms or a proper exercise of a dispositive power, not merely under trustees’ managerial tolerance.

See: Private residence and business reliefs

Event form

IHT100a–IHT100g identify the event category; the main account supplies parties, values, history and calculation information.

See: IHT reporting

Exclusions

A Schedule 3A exclusion can remove a non-taxable express trust from registration, but a later UK tax liability may still require registration.

See: Trust Registration Service

Express powers

Trustees or another power holder may appoint, advance, release or vary within the limits and purposes of the instrument.

See: Variation and termination

Express trust

Created intentionally by a settlor. Its terms normally identify the property, trustees and beneficial framework.

See: Types of trust and how they arise

F

Fiduciary loyalty

Trustees must not misuse powers, profit without authority or allow personal interests to displace the trust’s purposes.

See: Trustee duties and powers

Fiduciary responsibility

Trustees must act for proper purposes, address conflicts and must not treat trust property as their own.

See: Nature of a trust

FIG interaction

A qualifying new resident may claim relief for eligible attributed or matched foreign gains, with consequences for allowances and pool records.

See: Non-resident trust gains

Fixed interest

The beneficiary and quantum are determined by the instrument, even if enjoyment is postponed.

See: Beneficial interests

Formalities

A will, a declaration concerning land and particular asset transfers can require writing, signature, witnessing or registration.

See: Creation and validity

Forum

A governing-law clause does not always determine which court may hear a dispute or where enforcement is practical.

See: Governing law and jurisdiction

Fund ledger

Separate original property, additions by settlor, income, gains, accumulated income, loans, benefits and appointments.

See: Case analysis and worked examples

G

Gain matching

Sections 86 and 87 TCGA require chronological gains and capital-payment records, including onward gifts and unmatched pools.

See: Non-UK resident and offshore trusts

Governing law

Usually identified from the instrument and relevant choice-of-law rules; it governs matters such as validity, construction and administration.

See: Governing law and jurisdiction

H

Hold-over interaction

Section 226A can restrict Private Residence Relief after certain earlier section 260 hold-over claims.

See: Private residence and business reliefs

I

IHT classification

An interest created after 21 March 2006 is not automatically estate-linked; check whether it is an immediate post-death, disabled person’s or transitional serial interest.

See: Interest-in-possession trusts

IHT reliefs

Business and agricultural relief depend on current statutory conditions, ownership periods, activity and post-6 April 2026 limits.

See: Trust-owned businesses and property

IIP beneficiary

Allowable management expenses can reduce the income treated as belonging to the income beneficiary, with ordering rules across income types.

See: Trust management expenses

IIP income

The beneficiary is generally taxed on their trust-income entitlement with credit for tax paid by trustees, subject to expenses and mandates.

See: Beneficiary receipts

Immediate post-death interest

In Will and family trusts: A qualifying post-2006 interest in possession must satisfy statutory conditions; the label in the will is not conclusive.

In IHT and interests in possession: A post-2006 interest arising under a will or intestacy can qualify if the statutory conditions are met.

See: Will and family trusts; IHT and interests in possession

Incapacity

An attorney or deputy acts for an individual but does not automatically acquire trustee powers; separate offices and conflicts must be respected.

See: Death, divorce and incapacity

Income administration

Trustees receive income, deduct properly chargeable expenses and account for the balance, unless income is validly mandated directly.

See: Interest-in-possession trusts

Income and expenses

Trading profits, dividends, rents and finance costs have separate source rules and may belong to different trust funds or beneficiaries.

See: Trust-owned businesses and property

Income attribution

Settlor-interested rules and transfer-of-assets-abroad provisions can charge UK residents on foreign trust income, subject to statutory conditions and defences.

See: Non-UK resident and offshore trusts

Income or capital

The deed and trust law determine which fund bears an expense; tax rules do not automatically follow the accounting label.

See: Trust management expenses

Income Tax

HMRC’s general guidance treats the beneficiary as responsible for tax on bare-trust income.

See: Bare trusts and nominees

Interest in possession

In Beneficial interests: Broadly, an immediate right to current income or present enjoyment, subject to proper trust expenses. Tax statutes use additional qualifying categories.

In Who is taxable: Trustees commonly account at basic or dividend ordinary rates and the income beneficiary is charged on their entitlement, with credit for trustee tax.

See: Beneficial interests; Who is taxable

Investment

Trustees generally consider suitability and diversification and obtain proper advice unless reasonably unnecessary, subject to the deed and statutory regime.

See: Trustee duties and powers

L

Later transfer

Physical conveyance after the section 71 occasion is generally disregarded; the beneficiary takes the market-value base cost created then.

See: Creating and ending interests

Legal title

The trustees deal with third parties, safeguard the assets and exercise the powers attached to ownership, subject to the trust.

See: Nature of a trust

Letter of wishes

May guide trustees but is normally non-binding; trustees must exercise their own judgment under the deed.

See: Discretionary and accumulation trusts

Life tenant

Often entitled to net income or occupation during life, without an absolute right to the underlying capital.

See: Interest-in-possession trusts

Lifetime rate and grossing

Tax on value within the charge is normally at the lifetime rate; if the settlor bears tax, grossing can increase the transfer of value.

See: Transfers into trust

Living settlor

Foreign trust property is generally excluded only while the settlor is not long-term UK resident.

See: Foreign property from 6 April 2025

Long-term UK resident

Broadly, residence in at least 10 of the preceding 20 tax years brings worldwide property into scope, with a residence tail after departure that varies with history.

See: Foreign property from 6 April 2025

M

Managed Investment Entity

A trust whose financial assets are professionally managed can fall within the Investment Entity definition, subject to income and activity tests.

See: International reporting

Minimum composition

Retirement without replacement commonly requires at least two individual trustees or a trust corporation to remain, subject to the asset and governing rules.

See: Appointment and retirement of trustees

Minor children

Section 629 can attribute income to a parent-settlor where it belongs to or is paid or applied for an unmarried minor child; the £100 threshold must be tested per parent and child.

See: Settlements legislation

Minutes and resolutions

Record participants, quorum, declared conflicts, documents reviewed, decision and authority without unnecessary sensitive narrative.

See: Decision-making and records

N

Nominee function

The registered holder acts on instructions and deals with third parties, but beneficial ownership remains with the principal or beneficiary.

See: Bare trusts and nominees

Notice to file

A return is required when HMRC issues a notice; trustees also need to notify chargeability and register when the trust becomes liable.

See: Trust Self Assessment

O

Ongoing maintenance

Changes, missing mandatory data, closure and annual declaration obligations have distinct deadlines and penalty exposure.

See: Trust Registration Service

Only or main residence

The dwelling must genuinely be the beneficiary’s residence and the qualifying periods and restrictions must be computed.

See: Private residence and business reliefs

Ownership structure

Distinguish a business operated by trustees from shares, partnership interests or land merely held as investments.

See: Trust-owned businesses and property

P

Part 7A employment income

Third-party arrangements involving employee benefit trusts can create employment charges when relevant steps occur.

See: Anti-avoidance and disclosure

Passive NFE

If not a Financial Institution, a passive entity may need to disclose controlling persons to the institution maintaining its financial account.

See: International reporting

Payment and claims

Balancing tax is generally due 31 January, with payments on account where applicable; claims and elections have their own time limits.

See: Trust Self Assessment

Pension trust

Registered pension schemes have their own statutory tax framework and should not be analysed as an ordinary discretionary family trust.

See: Commercial and specialist trusts

People and powers

Track each settlor, trustee, beneficiary, protector and adviser, including residence, capacity, conflicts and authority.

See: Case analysis and worked examples

Permanent file

Executed instruments, variations, appointments, identity records, asset title, powers and key advice form the enduring record.

See: Governance and evidence

POAT interaction

The pre-owned-assets Income Tax charge has exclusions and double-charge provisions that require comparison with GWR treatment.

See: Settlor benefits and reservations

Pre-22 March 2006 interest

Generally remains within section 49 estate-linked treatment, subject to transitional changes and later events.

See: IHT and interests in possession

Principal charge

At a ten-year anniversary, trustees value relevant property and calculate an effective rate using the settlement history, nil-rate band, related settlements and chargeable additions.

See: Relevant-property regime

Proportionate charge

An exit rate uses the appropriate effective rate and complete quarters since commencement or the previous anniversary, subject to exceptions.

See: Relevant-property regime

Q

Qualifying IIP

Foreign property is excluded only if the relevant settlor test and the qualifying life tenant’s long-term-residence condition are satisfied.

See: Foreign property from 6 April 2025

R

Rate

Trust gains are generally charged at 24% in 2026/27, subject to specific reliefs and regimes.

See: Trustee chargeability

Recognition

The UK gives effect to the Hague Trusts Convention through the Recognition of Trusts Act 1987, subject to its scope and safeguards.

See: Governing law and jurisdiction

Reconciliation

TRS, tax returns, accounts, bank and custody statements and beneficiary certificates should describe the same events consistently.

See: Governance and evidence

Registration category

First determine whether the trust registers as taxable, as a registrable non-taxable express trust, or because of a UK land or business relationship.

See: Trust Registration Service

Relevant property

Broadly, settled property not within excluded property, a qualifying interest in possession or a statutory special-trust category.

See: Relevant-property regime

Relief and protection

Consent, release, limitation, exclusion clauses, court relief and trustee insurance require careful fact and legal analysis.

See: Breach of trust and remedies

Remainder or reversion

A future interest expected to take effect after an earlier interest ends. It may be vested or conditional.

See: Beneficial interests

Remainderman

Holds the succeeding capital interest, subject to the prior interest and any powers in the instrument.

See: Interest-in-possession trusts

Repayment position

A beneficiary’s personal rate may permit repayment of some credit in ordinary cases, but ring-fenced credits in settlor-interested or non-resident cases can behave differently.

See: Beneficiary receipts

Reportable interests

Settlors, beneficiaries, trustees or other persons can be account holders or controlling persons depending on classification and regime.

See: International reporting

Reservation of benefit

The donor must be excluded, or virtually excluded, from benefit and enjoyment during the relevant period.

See: Settlor benefits and reservations

Residence-based IHT

Foreign settled property status follows long-term residence tests from 6 April 2025, with deceased-settlor and qualifying-interest exceptions.

See: Non-UK resident and offshore trusts

Restoration and compensation

The court may require restoration of the fund or equitable compensation, with the measure depending on the duty and causal analysis.

See: Breach of trust and remedies

Restrictions

Settlor-interested trusts, non-residence, excluded property, relief interactions and non-business use can restrict or deny relief.

See: Hold-over relief

Resulting trust

May return or attribute beneficial ownership to a contributor or settlor when an intended disposition fails or the evidence supports retained beneficial ownership.

See: Types of trust and how they arise

Retained interest

Section 624 can attribute income where settlement property or income may benefit the settlor or their spouse or civil partner, subject to exceptions.

See: Settlements legislation

S

Section 165

Targets gifts of qualifying business assets and contains its own asset, company and restriction tests.

See: Hold-over relief

Section 260

Can apply where the disposal is a chargeable transfer for IHT purposes, even if no IHT is ultimately payable because of the nil-rate band or specified reliefs.

See: Hold-over relief

Settlement and settlor

The statutory concepts extend beyond formal trusts and can include bounteous arrangements. Identify the real provider of property or value.

See: Settlements legislation

Settlor

The person who intentionally creates an express trust and provides, or declares that they hold, its initial property. The settlor usually determines the trust terms and may also be a trustee or beneficiary, subject to the terms and applicable tax rules.

See: Nature of a trust

Settlor attribution

Section 86 can attribute gains of qualifying non-resident settlements to a UK-resident, domiciled or post-2025 in-scope settlor, subject to the detailed conditions.

See: Non-resident trust gains

Settlor-interested settlement

Income may be treated as the settlor’s under ITTOIA 2005, with trustee tax credited through the statutory mechanism.

See: Who is taxable

Single continuing body

TCGA 1992 generally treats trustees collectively, preserving acquisition history through trustee changes.

See: Trustee chargeability

Source computation

Compute each source under its own rules before combining trust income; capital receipts and sale proceeds are not income merely because cash is received.

See: Trust income

Source expenses

Expenses wholly and exclusively incurred for a rental business or trade are considered in that source computation, not again as trust-management expenses.

See: Trust management expenses

Special trust rates

Accumulation or discretionary income is generally charged at the special rates; interest-in-possession income uses different trustee rates.

See: Trust income

Specific charging rules

Settlements legislation and trust-gain matching often define the primary tax charge; they should be applied before assuming a general anti-avoidance rule.

See: Anti-avoidance and disclosure

Spouse gifts

The outright-gift exception has conditions and does not protect a gift wholly or substantially of a right to income.

See: Settlements legislation

Statutory trust

Arises because legislation imposes a trust, for example in particular succession, land or co-ownership contexts. The governing statute defines its incidents.

See: Types of trust and how they arise

Supplementary pages

SA901–SA906 cover trading, partnership, UK property, foreign income, capital gains and non-residence as applicable.

See: Trust Self Assessment

T

Tax events

Appointments and changes can trigger CGT disposals, IHT exit or transfer-of-value analysis, Income Tax effects and TRS updates.

See: Variation and termination

Tax framework

Trustees may face special Income Tax rates and a tax-pool mechanism, while relevant-property IHT charges commonly require separate review.

See: Discretionary and accumulation trusts

Tax matrix

For each event test Income Tax, CGT, IHT, SDLT, VAT, TRS, AEOI and reporting without allowing one answer to dictate another.

See: Case analysis and worked examples

Tax pool

Tracks specified trustee Income Tax available to support credits on discretionary income payments; it is a memorandum, not a bank account.

See: Trust income

Tax residence

In Governing law and jurisdiction: Trustee residence and statutory tax rules are not determined solely by the deed?s governing law or the location of a bank account.

In Appointment and retirement of trustees: Changing trustees can alter the trust?s residence or create migration consequences, so tax review must precede execution.

See: Governing law and jurisdiction; Appointment and retirement of trustees

Termination on death

Special no-gain/no-loss rules can apply when certain interests in possession end on death; classify the interest before applying them.

See: Creating and ending interests

Three certainties

The words must show a binding trust intention; the trust property must be identifiable; and the beneficiaries or permitted purpose must satisfy the applicable certainty test.

See: Creation and validity

Tracing and proprietary claims

Beneficiaries may seek substituted property or value, subject to evidential rules and third-party defences.

See: Breach of trust and remedies

Transfer into trust

A gift normally uses market value between connected or non-arm’s-length parties, subject to any valid hold-over relief.

See: Creating and ending interests

Transfer of assets abroad

Can charge UK-resident individuals connected with transfers producing income abroad, subject to detailed benefit, motive and EU-related provisions.

See: Anti-avoidance and disclosure

Transfer of value

IHT starts with the reduction in the settlor’s estate, not automatically the asset’s stand-alone value.

See: Transfers into trust

Transitional serial interest

A narrow statutory category for successor interests following qualifying pre-existing interests.

See: IHT and interests in possession

Trust accounts

Reconcile legal ownership and cash movements while preserving income, capital, tax-pool and individual-fund distinctions.

See: Decision-making and records

Trust property

The assets must be identifiable. New property, substituted assets and accumulated income may become part of the trust fund.

See: Nature of a trust

Trustee residence

In Trustee chargeability: Apply the statutory trustee-residence rules for the tax year; governing law and asset location do not decide residence.

In Non-UK resident and offshore trusts: Income Tax and CGT residence depends on trustee composition and, in some cases, the settlor?s status when the trust was created or funded.

See: Trustee chargeability; Non-UK resident and offshore trusts

Trustee

A person or eligible legal body that holds or controls trust property and must administer it in accordance with the trust terms and applicable law. Trustees owe fiduciary duties and must not treat trust property as their own.

See: Nature of a trust

Trustee-documented trust

Where conditions are met, a reporting Financial Institution trustee undertakes reporting, but the trust has its own mandatory registration considerations.

See: International reporting

V

Valuation

Use the event-date open-market value, liabilities and relief evidence, including related-property and shareholding effects.

See: IHT reporting

Valuations

Use an appropriate valuation date, basis and qualified evidence where tax, fairness or dispositive decisions depend on value.

See: Decision-making and records

Variation after death

Deeds of variation and section 144 appointments can obtain specified retrospective IHT or CGT treatment only if statutory conditions and time limits are met.

See: Death, divorce and incapacity

Vesting

A deed alone may not update title to land, shares, accounts or foreign assets; separate registrations and transfers can be required.

See: Appointment and retirement of trustees

W

Will trust

The will defines the trustees, powers and interests, but tax provisions may treat events from death or from later administration dates differently.

See: Will and family trusts

Y

Younger beneficiaries

Bereaved-minor and age 18-to-25 regimes have detailed statutory conditions and different IHT consequences.

See: Will and family trusts
