Trust administration — evidence of proper governance

Overview

Trustee records should show what authority was used, which facts and advice were considered, how conflicts were managed and how implementation matched the decision. Accounts must distinguish trust funds, income, capital, liabilities and beneficiary transactions.

A reliable audit trail links minutes and resolutions, trust accounts, contemporaneous valuations, and disciplined document control.

Building a defensible decision record

Minutes and resolutions

Record participants, quorum, declared conflicts, documents reviewed, decision and authority without unnecessary sensitive narrative.

Analyse the substance rather than the label. Read the trust instrument with the applicable legislation and current official guidance, identify the person whose rights or obligations are affected, and distinguish the trust-law answer from each tax and reporting question. The file should state the effective date, asset or amount involved and evidence supporting the conclusion. Once that position is recorded, it can be carried into the analysis of trust accounts without assuming that the same legal or tax classification applies.

Practical issues and safeguards

Minutes should evidence a real process, not retrospectively manufacture one. Excessive disclosure can also harm confidentiality; record sufficient reasons and retain privileged advice appropriately.

  • Create contemporaneous minutes identifying power, facts, advice, conflicts and decision-makers.
  • Link deeds, valuations, accounts, tax records and completion evidence to the resolution.
  • Protect privilege and restrict beneficiary data to what is necessary.

With those safeguards recorded, the illustration below brings the relevant findings together and tests whether the documents, chronology and proposed treatment remain consistent.

Illustration

Trustees approve a capital appointment to one of four discretionary beneficiaries. The file should identify the power, relevant beneficiary circumstances, conflicts, equality considerations, valuation, tax effects, formal deed and evidence that the correct asset was transferred.

Applied analysis: Build the file before appointment, record why the beneficiary is selected, execute the deed, reconcile the transfer and retain valuation and tax evidence.
