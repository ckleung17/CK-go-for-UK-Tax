Private residence and business reliefs

Capital Gains Tax — relief through a beneficiary

Tax treatment

Study checkpoint: 2026/27. Trustees do not obtain relief merely because a beneficiary lives in trust property or operates a business. Section 225 Private Residence Relief requires occupation under an entitlement conferred by the settlement, and business-related reliefs impose their own beneficiary, asset, company and timing conditions.

The review must evidence the beneficiary’s entitlement to occupy and use as an only or main residence, while separately testing any hold-over interaction and relevant business reliefs.

Connecting occupation, business use and relief

Entitlement to occupy

The beneficiary must occupy under the settlement terms or a proper exercise of a dispositive power, not merely under trustees’ managerial tolerance.

Analyse the substance rather than the label. Read the trust instrument with the applicable legislation and current official guidance, identify the person whose rights or obligations are affected, and distinguish the trust-law answer from each tax and reporting question. Practical issues and safeguards

Charging rent does not necessarily prevent section 225 relief, while rent-free occupation does not guarantee it. The legal source of the occupation entitlement is critical.

  • Evidence the beneficiary’s legal occupation entitlement and actual main-residence facts.
  • Map acquisition, occupation, absence, letting and disposal periods.
  • Test hold-over restrictions and business relief conditions separately from occupation.
