Trust Analysis Framework

A single method for legal, tax and administration questions

Home Trust Index

Purpose

Use this framework before applying a topic-specific rule. A trust question should be analysed in a fixed order: establish the legal relationship and facts first, identify each relevant event and capacity, apply each tax separately, and finish with reporting, evidence and review. Do not begin with the desired tax result.

Core analysis sequence

  1. Define the question. Record the jurisdiction, governing law, relevant tax year or event date, and the decision or filing that is required.
  2. Collect the authority. Obtain the executed deed, will, court order or statutory provision, together with later appointments, variations, resolutions and professional advice.
  3. Map the people and capacities. Identify every settlor, trustee, beneficiary, protector, appointor, personal representative and person holding consent or control powers. Record residence, capacity and changes over time.
  4. Map the property and money flows. Trace the original fund, later additions, substitutions, income, gains, expenses, loans, benefits and distributions. Reconcile legal title with accounts and third-party records.
  5. Establish the trust-law position. Test validity, beneficial interests, trustee authority, fiduciary duties, governing law and whether any event creates, varies or ends an interest.
  6. Build the chronology. Put every transfer, appointment, death, residence change, disposal and payment in date order. Mark facts as evidenced, assumed, disputed or missing.
  7. Apply each tax separately. Determine the taxpayer, charging provision, amount, relief and timing for Income Tax, CGT, IHT and any other relevant tax. Do not allow one tax conclusion to dictate another.
  8. Prepare reproducible calculations. Show valuations, rates, thresholds, exchange rates, prior events and assumptions. Reconcile trustee computations with beneficiary statements and the trust accounts.
  9. Complete compliance analysis. Identify the responsible person, registration or return, payment, deadline, disclosure, beneficial-owner information and supporting evidence.
  10. Conclude and review. State the answer, alternatives, sensitivities and unresolved facts. Distinguish legislation from HMRC interpretation and refer disputed, cross-border or high-value matters for specialist human review.

Stage-specific checkpoints

Stage 1: legal foundations

  • Test intention, certainty, formalities, constitution and capacity.
  • Separate legal title, beneficial interests and the powers attached to each role.
  • Identify resulting, constructive or statutory trusts only from the applicable facts and law.

Stage 2: structures and uses

  • Classify the trust from its operative terms, not its informal name.
  • Identify fixed, income, discretionary, remainder and default interests.
  • Explain the practical purpose and test whether specialist statutory conditions apply.

Stage 3: trusteeship and administration

  • Locate the power supporting each appointment, investment, delegation or distribution.
  • Check trustee composition, conflicts, decision procedure, advice and record keeping.
  • Complete vesting and third-party registration rather than relying only on a signed deed.

Stage 4: Income Tax

  • Compute income by source before applying the rules for the trust type.
  • Test settlor attribution, beneficiary entitlement, discretionary payments and available tax credits.
  • Reconcile trustee returns, tax-pool records and beneficiary statements.

Stage 5: Capital Gains Tax

  • Identify the disposal, deemed disposal, taxpayer and market-value rule.
  • Test absolute entitlement, hold-over, residence, attribution and beneficiary matching separately.
  • Track deferred gains and later events so relief is not counted twice or lost.

Stage 6: Inheritance Tax

  • Classify the transfer and settled property at the event date.
  • Reconstruct earlier transfers, additions, related settlements, relief history and prior charges.
  • Calculate entry, anniversary, exit or death consequences using the law and values applicable at that date.

Stage 7: registration and reporting

  • Test TRS, Self Assessment, IHT event reporting and international reporting independently.
  • Maintain current beneficial-owner, residence and controlling-person information.
  • Record filing evidence, payment references, amendments and statutory deadlines.

Stage 8: advanced and cross-border cases

  • Prepare one integrated chronology but retain separate legal and tax analyses.
  • Check residence, treaty, foreign-law, attribution, matching and anti-avoidance rules before quantifying.
  • Show alternative outcomes where documents, valuations or residence facts remain uncertain.

Use with care

This framework is a study and file-review aid, not tax or legal advice. Rules, rates, deadlines and HMRC practice must be checked for the relevant jurisdiction and event date.
