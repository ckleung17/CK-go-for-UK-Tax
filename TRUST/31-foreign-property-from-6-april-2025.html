IHT framework

Study checkpoint: chargeable events in 2026/27. For chargeable events from 6 April 2025, the excluded-property status of foreign settled property generally moves with the settlor’s long-term UK residence rather than being fixed by domicile when the asset entered trust. Deceased-settlor and qualifying-interest cases require additional tests.

For each chargeable event, identify whether the settlor is a long-term UK resident, whether the relevant person is a living settlor or deceased settlor, and whether a beneficiary holds a qualifying IIP.

Applying the post-2025 foreign-property rules

Long-term UK resident

Broadly, residence in at least 10 of the preceding 20 tax years brings worldwide property into scope, with a residence tail after departure that varies with history.

Deceased settlor

If death occurred from 6 April 2025, status generally follows long-term residence immediately before death; pre-6 April 2025 deaths retain a domicile-based test for settled property.

Practical issues and safeguards

This area is transitional and fact-sensitive. Trustee residence is not the test for excluded foreign property, and a FIG claim does not exempt trust property from IHT.

  • Record settlor residence history and status at each relevant post-2025 charge date.
  • Classify property location and any qualifying interest without using trustee residence as a shortcut.
  • Keep pre-2025 facts and transitional events available for later anniversaries and exits.
