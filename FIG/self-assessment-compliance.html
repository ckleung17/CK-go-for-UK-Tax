Self-Assessment Mechanisms: Reporting via SA106 & SA109

A technical guide to navigating the "report then relieve" filing requirements introduced under the modern FIG framework.

The "Report Then Relieve" Framework

The FIG regime completely replaces the privacy mechanisms of the historical remittance basis. Under the old system, unremitted offshore funds could frequently be kept entirely off the UK tax return. Under the current residence-based model, compliance operates strictly as a "Report Then Relieve" process.

Taxpayers must fully calculate and declare their worldwide unremitted profile on their primary schedules on an arising basis. After declaring these figures, they apply a secondary statutory overlay to wipe out the calculated UK tax liabilities.

Full systemic filing parameters can be verified directly via HMRC Self Assessment Helpsheet HS266.

Step-by-Step Schedule Disclosures

A compliant FIG claim requires coordinating three distinct elements of the Self-Assessment tax return:

Step 1: Declare the Totals (SA106 / SA108)

All foreign income (e.g., offshore interest, dividends, rental income) must first be broken down by source country and listed inside the standard SA106 (Foreign) supplementary pages. If foreign assets were sold during the year, the gross proceeds and calculated gains must be detailed within the SA108 (Capital Gains Summary) pages.

Step 2: Submit the FIG Claim (SA109)

Once everything is declared on the arising schedules, the actual claim to remove the tax liability is processed through the SA109 (Residence and FIG regime etc.) pages. The specific boxes on the SA109 form must be populated:

  • Box 28: Place an 'X' in this box to make a formal claim for relief on foreign income.
  • Box 29: Place an 'X' in this box to make a formal claim for relief on foreign capital gains.
  • Box 30: Place an 'X' in this box if you hold UK income or gains deemed to be foreign under qualifying asset holding company (QAHC) rules.

Review the structural specifications for these boxes via HMRC SA109 Supplementary Pages & Notes.

Step 3: Matching Adjustments

The taxpayer must apply a relief amount exactly matching the eligible foreign figures declared on their return. This ensures the automated calculation system scales back the tax due on those specific sections to zero.

Filing Deadlines: For any given tax year, the formal FIG claim must be submitted by the standard digital filing deadline of 31 January following the end of that tax year, or within the standard 12-month amendment window ending on 31 January of the subsequent year.
