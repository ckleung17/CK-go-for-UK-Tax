Step 2 of the Income Tax Calculation

Section 23 of the Income Tax Act 2007 sets out the stages used to calculate an individual's Income Tax liability. At Step 1, the amounts charged to Income Tax are identified and added together as total income. At Step 2, qualifying deductions and reliefs are subtracted from the relevant income components to calculate net income.

OWR example

Employment income included at Step 1: £200,000

Less OWR claimed at Step 2: (£25,000)

Net employment income: £175,000

OWR is therefore an income deduction, not a tax credit. A £25,000 OWR claim reduces the employment income on which tax is calculated by £25,000; it does not reduce the final tax bill by £25,000. The remaining net income then passes through the later stages of the calculation, including the application of allowances and tax rates.

For adjusted net income purposes, OWR is disregarded. The relieved foreign employment income is therefore brought back into account when determining matters such as the High Income Child Benefit Charge and eligibility for Tax-Free Childcare.

See HMRC Employment Income Manual EIM43580 for HMRC's calculation and example.
