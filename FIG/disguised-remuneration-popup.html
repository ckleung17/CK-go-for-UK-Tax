Disguised Remuneration Rules

Part 7A ITEPA 2003 contains anti-avoidance rules for employment rewards provided through a third party, such as a trust or other intermediary. They are intended to prevent earnings from escaping or deferring Income Tax merely because the employee receives value indirectly instead of as ordinary salary or a bonus.

A taxable amount may arise where, in connection with an employment, a third party takes a relevant step such as:

  • making a loan or payment to the employee or a connected person;
  • earmarking money or assets for the employee's benefit; or
  • making an asset available or transferring an asset.

Example

Instead of paying a £50,000 bonus directly, an employer contributes the amount to an employee benefit trust, which then advances £50,000 to the employee as a loan that is not genuinely intended to be repaid. If Part 7A applies, the relevant amount is treated as employment income rather than as a tax-free loan.

Not every payment, loan or benefit involving a third party is caught. Part 7A contains detailed exclusions, including exclusions for many ordinary commercial transactions and registered pension arrangements. The facts and the specific statutory conditions must therefore be checked.

For OWR purposes, a Part 7A amount can be qualifying foreign employment income only to the extent that it relates to duties performed outside the UK during a qualifying year and the other OWR conditions are satisfied.

See HMRC Employment Income Manual EIM45000 onwards for detailed guidance.
