The 4-Year Tax Holiday & Qualifying New Resident (QNR) Criteria

A technical analysis of the qualifying criteria for new arrivals claiming a 100% exemption on Foreign Income and Gains.

Defining a Qualifying New Resident (QNR)

An individual is a Qualifying New Resident for a tax year only if both cumulative conditions below are satisfied. Eligibility is determined by tax residence rather than domicile, nationality or an intention to remain permanently in the UK.

The Cumulative Eligibility Test

  • Current residence and four-year window: The individual must be UK tax resident under the Statutory Residence Test (SRT), and the relevant tax year must fall within the four consecutive tax years beginning with their first UK-resident tax year.
  • Previous non-residence: Immediately before that first UK-resident tax year, the individual must have been non-UK resident for at least 10 consecutive tax years. This rule is codified in Finance Act 2025, Schedule 1.

Important: Both conditions must be satisfied. A non-resident year during the four-year window does not extend the window, and relief must be claimed separately for each qualifying year. Members of the House of Commons or House of Lords cannot claim the relief.

For official validation procedures, refer to HMRC's interactive checker tool: Check eligibility for the 4-year FIG regime.

The Mechanics of the Four-Year FIG Window

The regime operates through a fixed window of four consecutive tax years beginning with the individual's first UK-resident tax year. It is not a selection of four years in which claims may be made.

The Window Runs for Four Consecutive Tax Years

The first UK-resident tax year is Year 1, followed by Years 2, 3 and 4. A claim is required for each year and for each amount of eligible foreign income or gains that the individual chooses to relieve. Choosing not to claim in one year does not preserve that year for later use.

If the individual's first UK-resident tax year began before the regime commenced on 6 April 2025, the four-year window still starts with that earlier year; it does not restart in 2025/26. For example, an eligible individual whose first UK-resident year was 2022/23 can claim only for 2025/26, the fourth and final year of the window (HMRC eligibility guidance).

Crucial Trap: The window does not pause during a temporary period of non-UK residence. If an individual is UK resident in Year 1, non-UK resident in Year 2 and UK resident again in Year 3, no claim can be made for Year 2. The individual may claim for any qualifying years remaining within the original window, but Year 2 cannot be deferred, replaced or carried forward.

What Can Be Relieved?

For each qualifying tax year, a Qualifying New Resident may claim 100% relief on eligible foreign income and qualifying foreign gains identified in the claim. HMRC Helpsheet HS266 explains the claim procedure and the categories that qualify.

  • Qualifying foreign income: Examples include dividends from non-UK resident companies, profits of an overseas property business, foreign interest and profits of a trade carried on wholly outside the UK. Foreign employment income is subject to the separate Overseas Workday Relief rules.
  • Qualifying foreign gains: Gains realised on disposals of qualifying foreign assets, subject to the statutory asset and UK-land conditions described in HMRC CG26400.

Claim required: Relief is not automatic. The individual must report the relevant income and gains and quantify the relief claimed for each source or gain on the appropriate Self Assessment pages.

Remitting FIG-Relieved Funds to the UK

A key benefit of the regime is that qualifying foreign income and gains covered by a valid FIG claim may be brought to the UK without an additional tax charge arising merely because of the remittance.

Remittance rule: Qualifying foreign income and gains that arise in a qualifying tax year and are included in a FIG relief claim may be remitted during or after the four-year window without a further UK tax liability solely as a result of bringing those relieved amounts to the UK.

Scope: This treatment applies only to amounts covered by a valid FIG claim. Foreign income and gains arising before 6 April 2025 fall outside the four-year FIG regime and generally remain subject to the legacy remittance rules. Eligible former remittance-basis users may instead designate and remit qualifying amounts under the Temporary Repatriation Facility (TRF).

For the claim procedure, see HMRC Helpsheet HS266 and HMRC RFIG42100.
