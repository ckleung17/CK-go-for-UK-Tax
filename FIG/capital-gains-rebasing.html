Capital Gains Tax Rebasing Rules for Long-Held Offshore Assets

A technical review of the transitional valuation adjustments designed to shield historical growth from the arising basis.

The 5 April 2017 Valuation Break

To prevent the sudden, unfair taxation of capital growth that occurred long before the abolition of the remittance basis, Finance Act 2025 introduced a vital transitional relief. For qualifying individuals disposing of long-held foreign capital assets on or after 6 April 2025, the acquisition cost can be stepped up to the asset's fair market value on **5 April 2017**.

Important Legislative Timeline Alert: The original policy draft published in the Spring Budget 2024 proposed a rebasing date of 5 April 2019. However, following the Autumn Budget, the government officially rolled the rebasing window back to 5 April 2017.

Administrative filing parameters can be verified directly via HMRC Helpsheet HS266 (Section 6: Rebasing Personal Foreign Capital Assets).

Four Statutory Conditions for Eligibility

A taxpayer cannot freely choose to rebase an asset unless they satisfy four strict criteria concurrently:

1. Personal Holding Requirement

The asset must have been owned by the individual personally on 5 April 2017 and held continuously until the date of the eventual disposal. Assets held inside non-UK corporate wrapper companies or foreign trust structures are completely disqualified from this specific relief.

2. Domicile Exclusion History

The individual must not have been domiciled or deemed domiciled in the UK at any point prior to the 2025/26 tax year. Long-term residents who had already hit the historical 15-out-of-20-years residency threshold before 2025 cannot use this adjustment.

3. Historical Claim Requirement

The individual must have made a formal, valid claim to use the remittance basis for at least one tax year between 6 April 2017 and 5 April 2025.

CPD Trap: Years where an individual automatically qualified for the remittance basis without a formal application (such as having unremitted foreign income under £2,000) do not satisfy this condition.

4. Foreign Situs Window

The asset must be situated outside the UK from 6 March 2024 to 5 April 2025. Temporary imports or relocations of tangible property within this window face scrutiny.
