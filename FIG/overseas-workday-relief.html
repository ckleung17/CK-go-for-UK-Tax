Modernized Overseas Workday Relief (OWR) under the FIG Framework

An exploration of the updated rules, statutory income caps, and the decoupling of the offshore remittance restriction.

The Shift from Remittance to Exemption

Overseas Workday Relief (OWR) was historically available to non-domiciled employees who claimed the remittance basis. Effective 6 April 2025, the framework was completely overhauled by Finance Act 2025.

The modernized OWR framework is codified under sections 41M to 41T of ITEPA 2003. It has transitioned from a remittance-based mechanism into an **arising-based exemption system**.

Technical operational parameters can be monitored via HMRC Helpsheet HS211 (Employment, Residence, and Domicile Issues).

Key Architectural Mechanics

The revised framework introduces three profound operational shifts for globally mobile executives:

1. Complete Remittance Freedom

Historically, employees were forced to pay their foreign earnings into complex, qualifying offshore bank accounts. If any money was transferred to or spent in the UK, the relief was instantly lost.

The Modern Rule: The requirement to retain funds offshore has been entirely repealed. Relief is granted based solely on where the duties were performed. Qualifying employees can now have their overseas payroll paid straight into a UK bank account and spend it immediately with zero tax impact.

2. Alignment with the 4-Year Window

The eligibility timeframe for OWR has been expanded from its historical 3-year threshold to match the full **4-year FIG window**. To claim, an individual must be a Qualifying New Resident (QNR) who was a non-UK tax resident for at least 10 consecutive tax years prior to arrival.

3. Introduction of the Statutory Monetary Cap

To prevent high-earning executives from completely sheltering multi-million-pound salaries, the reformed relief introduces a strict statutory cap under section 41R of ITEPA 2003. The maximum amount of employment income that can be exempted under OWR is now limited to the lower of:

  • £300,000 per tax year.
  • 30% of the individual's total qualifying employment income.
