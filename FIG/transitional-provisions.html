Transitional Rules for Pre-2025 Arrivals & The 50% Income Policy Shift

An analysis of the structural bridging rules for existing remittance basis users transitioning into the arising basis framework.

Tail-End FIG Eligibility for Existing Residents

The introduction of the Foreign Income and Gains (FIG) regime on 6 April 2025 did not completely exclude individuals who arrived in the UK prior to that date. If an individual was within their first 4 consecutive tax years of UK residence on 6 April 2025, they can claim FIG relief for the remaining balance of those 4 years.

The Entry Windows for Pre-2025 Arrivals:

  • Arrived in 2022/23: Eligible to claim FIG relief for 1 year only (the 2025/26 tax year).
  • Arrived in 2023/24: Eligible to claim FIG relief for 2 years (2025/26 and 2026/27).
  • Arrived in 2024/25: Eligible to claim FIG relief for 3 years (2025/26 through 2027/28).

CPD Entry Prerequisite: To qualify for this remaining window, the individual must have satisfied the core 10-year prior non-residence rule at the original time of their arrival.

Statutory parameters mapping tail-end windows are set down in Finance Act 2025, Schedule 1. Complete practical steps are reviewed inside HMRC Helpsheet HS266.

Critical Split Year Rule: If an individual arrived in the UK partway through a tax year prior to 6 April 2025 and qualified for Split Year Treatment under the Statutory Residence Test (SRT), the entire tax year still counts as one full year of their 4-year window. Even though their foreign income generated during the "overseas part" of that arrival year was protected from UK tax under standard residency rules, the clock is not prorated. A partial year of residence completely consumes Year 1 of eligibility, meaning their remaining time under the new FIG regime will be shorter than expected.

Study Guidance: For a comprehensive breakdown of how these transitional timelines interact with older unremitted funds, mixed fund rules, and the Temporary Repatriation Facility (TRF), please progress to Topic 2: The 4-Year Tax Holiday.

The 50% Income Reduction Policy Reversal

A vital timeline element for CPD records is the evolution of the transitional protections concerning long-term remittance basis users who did not qualify for the new 4-year holiday.

The Original Policy Proposal

Initially, a transitional clause was proposed to ease the financial impact of moving to an arising basis. This clause stated that individuals moving from the remittance basis to the arising basis on 6 April 2025 would only pay UK tax on 50% of their foreign income during the 2025/26 tax year.

The Statutory Reality (The Policy Scrapped)

Following the Autumn Budget, the government officially **scrapped** the 50% foreign income exemption clause.

LEGISLATION ALERT: The 50% temporary concession was removed. From 6 April 2025, all existing remittance basis users who fall outside the 4-year FIG window are taxed on 100% of their worldwide income and capital gains on the standard arising basis.

The removal of this transitional relief is codified within Finance Act 2025. For contextual background notes, review the HMRC Policy Revisions Technical Note.
