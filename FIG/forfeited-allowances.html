The Financial Trade-offs: Forfeiting Personal Allowances & Capital Losses

A critical assessment of the mandatory statutory forfeitures required when electing into the 4-year FIG regime.

The Statutory Cost of FIG Relief

Claiming relief under the Foreign Income and Gains (FIG) regime is not an automatic or cost-free decision. For every tax year a taxpayer checks Box 28 or Box 29 on schedule SA109, they trigger an absolute, mandatory forfeiture of their baseline UK tax allowances.

This requirement replicates the punitive trade-offs found in the historical remittance basis framework. As a result, claiming FIG relief is a deliberate annual planning choice rather than a default position.

The complete index of restricted statutory code provisions is detailed in Finance Act 2025, Schedule 1. Administrative directions regarding these restrictions are outlined in HMRC Helpsheet HS266 (Section 5: Allowances You'll Lose).

Deep Dive: What is Sacrificed?

When an annual FIG election is finalized, the following structural mechanisms are completely dismantled for that tax year:

1. The UK Income Tax Personal Allowance

Taxpayers lose their standard UK Personal Allowance (£12,570). Every pound of UK-sourced income—such as employment income, UK bank interest, or UK rental profits—becomes taxable from the very first penny at the taxpayer's marginal rates.

2. The Capital Gains Tax Annual Exempt Amount

The Capital Gains Tax (CGT) Annual Exempt Amount (£3,000) is entirely forfeited. Any taxable UK capital gains realized during the year are taxed in full without the benefit of a tax-free baseline band.

3. Foreign Capital Losses Restriction

The Asymmetry Trap: While capital gains realized on offshore assets are exempted from UK tax under a valid FIG claim, any capital losses realized on offshore assets during a claim year become completely unusable. These foreign capital losses cannot be offset against taxable UK capital gains, nor can they be carried forward to subsequent tax years.

4. Ancillary Allowances

The forfeiture rules also strip away entitlement to the Blind Person's Allowance, Married Couple's Allowance, and the Transferable Marriage Allowance.
