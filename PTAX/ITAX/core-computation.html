ITA 2007 section 23 structure

  1. Identify total income under each source.
  2. Apply reliefs deductible at Step 2 to find net income.
  3. Deduct allowances at Step 3, allocated under section 25 to produce the greatest reduction, subject to the rules.
  4. Calculate tax at the applicable non-savings, savings, dividend and devolved rates.
  5. Add amounts charged at special rates where required.
  6. Deduct tax reductions in the statutory order.
  7. Add any further statutory amounts and deduct tax treated as paid or tax credits to reach liability.

Under ITA 2007 s.16, savings and dividends generally form the highest slices, with dividends above savings. Particular provisions can override normal ordering.

Main rates

For England, Wales and Northern Ireland, the 2026/27 taxable slices are: first £37,700 at 20%; £37,701 to £125,140 at 40%; and excess at 45%. Scottish non-savings, non-dividend income uses separate rates.

Control points

  • Do not confuse total income, net income, adjusted net income and taxable income.
  • 0% savings and dividend bands consume rate-band capacity.
  • Gift Aid and relief-at-source pensions can extend rate limits.
  • Tax deducted at source and tax treated as paid are not deductions from income.
  • Tax reducers cannot normally create a repayment beyond their statutory effect.

Sources

ITA 2007 s.23; ITA 2007 s.25; HMRC 2026/27 rates. Checked 18 July 2026.
