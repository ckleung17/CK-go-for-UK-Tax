Grossing up

A qualifying net Gift Aid donation or relief-at-source pension contribution is normally grossed up at the 20% basic rate: divide the net payment by 80%. Thus £800 net is £1,000 gross.

Computation effects

  • The gross amount is deducted in calculating adjusted net income under the applicable rules.
  • Gift Aid and relief-at-source pension contributions extend the basic- and higher-rate limits by the gross amount.
  • The extension can generate higher- or additional-rate relief through the liability computation.
  • Relief does not turn a non-taxpayer’s donation into a risk-free transaction: the donor must satisfy the Gift Aid tax condition.

Net pay, relief at source and salary sacrifice are different pension mechanisms. Pension annual-allowance and earnings limits belong to the pensions study note and must also be checked.

Sources

ITA 2007 s.58; HMRC adjusted net income; HMRC Gift Aid. Checked 18 July 2026.
