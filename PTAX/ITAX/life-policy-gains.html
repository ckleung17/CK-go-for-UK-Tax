Life-policy gains and top-slicing relief

Home Income Tax Capital Gains Tax PAYE National Insurance Pensions Tax

Chargeable events

Death, maturity, full surrender, assignments for money or money’s worth and excess part surrenders can create chargeable-event gains under ITTOIA 2005. The taxable gain is not necessarily the economic profit or cash withdrawn. UK and foreign policies can have different tax-treated-as-paid and residence consequences.

Place in the computation

A gain is generally savings income and the full gain enters total and adjusted net income. It can affect the Personal Allowance, savings allowances and other income-linked charges. A UK-policy gain may carry basic-rate tax treated as paid; confirm the certificate and statutory conditions.

Top-slicing relief

For an individual, top-slicing relief may reduce the additional liability caused by bringing several years’ growth into one year. It compares the tax effect of the full gain with a notional annual slice. The number of complete years, allowance recalculation, relief ordering and any time-apportioned reduction are critical.

Top-slicing relief is a tax reducer, not a division of the taxable gain. It is not generally available to trustees or personal representatives.

Sources

ITTOIA 2005 Part 4 Chapter 9; HMRC IPTM3820; HMRC HS320. Checked 18 July 2026. Complex cases require human review.
