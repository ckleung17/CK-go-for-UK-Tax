Trust beneficiaries

The amount and character entering a beneficiary’s computation depends on the trust type and distribution. Bare-trust income is generally the beneficiary’s income. Interest-in-possession and discretionary distributions can carry different tax credits and gross amounts. Settlor-interested rules can instead charge the settlor.

Deceased estates

A specific legacy does not automatically produce taxable income for the beneficiary. A residuary beneficiary may be taxed on estate income arising during administration, supported by information from the personal representatives. Estate income and the underlying asset transfer must be distinguished.

Workflow

  1. Identify the legal capacity and beneficial entitlement.
  2. Obtain the R185 or equivalent statement and reconcile gross income and tax credit.
  3. Classify savings, dividend and other income for stacking.
  4. Check settlements, foreign-income and residence interactions.

This page is an individual-computation bridge. Trustee and personal-representative liabilities belong to the Trust study note and require separate calculation.
