Why the measure matters

Adjusted net income affects the Personal Allowance taper above £100,000 and the High Income Child Benefit Charge above its applicable threshold. It can also be relevant to eligibility for childcare support and must not be confused with pension “threshold income” or “adjusted income”.

Method

  1. Start with net income from all taxable sources.
  2. Deduct the gross amount of qualifying Gift Aid.
  3. Deduct applicable gross pension contributions, including grossed-up relief-at-source payments.
  4. Make the remaining statutory adjustments, including specified add-backs.

The Personal Allowance taper produces an effective marginal Income Tax rate above the headline rate while allowance is withdrawn. Savings, dividends, property income, trust income and chargeable-event gains can all push adjusted net income across a threshold.

HICBC, Tax-Free Childcare and pension annual-allowance decisions are separate regimes with their own definitions and claim mechanics.

Sources

ITA 2007 s.58; HMRC adjusted net income; HMRC HICBC. Checked 18 July 2026.
