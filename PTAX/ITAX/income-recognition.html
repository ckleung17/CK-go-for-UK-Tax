Income recognition

After classifying an amount, apply the recognition rule for that source to identify the tax year in which it enters the Income Tax computation.

Cash basis vs. accruals basis

  • Under a cash basis, business receipts and allowable expenses are generally recognised when money is received or paid. An invoice date does not normally determine the year if payment occurs later.
  • Under the accruals basis, income and expenses are generally recognised when they are earned or incurred. Amounts owed at the year end, payments in advance and work in progress may therefore require adjustments.

These are principally methods for calculating business profits. They do not replace the separate recognition rules for employment income, pensions, trust income or estate income.

Recognition rules for non-savings income sources

General recognition guide for an individual in 2026/27
Income source How the tax year is identified General recognition rule Important qualification
Employment earnings When earnings are received or treated as received Money earnings are normally taxed in the year they are treated as received. This is generally the earlier of actual payment and the date the employee becomes entitled to payment. Separate rules apply to non-money earnings and some securities-related income.
Company directors’ remuneration When remuneration is received or treated as received The ordinary employment receipt rules apply, together with additional rules that can treat remuneration as received before it is paid. Receipt can arise when remuneration is credited in the company’s accounts or records, when the amount is determined after the end of the period for which it is payable, or at another deemed-receipt event. The precise event and date must be checked.
Trading profits Cash basis by default; accruals basis where elected or required Eligible unincorporated businesses use the cash basis by default unless they elect to calculate profits under generally accepted accounting practice (GAAP) or are excluded from the cash basis. The tax-year basis is separate from the accounting method. Profits arising from 6 April 2026 to 5 April 2027 are taxed for 2026/27. Accounts with another year end may need profits from more than one accounting period to be apportioned.
Property income Cash basis by default for qualifying businesses; GAAP where required or elected The cash basis is the default for most individual or partnership property businesses with qualifying receipts not exceeding the applicable limit. Rent is then generally recognised when received, including receipt by an agent. The accruals basis applies where required or elected. It allocates rent and expenses to the periods in which they are earned or incurred rather than simply following payment dates.
Pension income Amounts accruing for the tax year Most pension income is charged by reference to the amount accruing for the tax year. The amount received often matches the amount accruing, but arrears or advance payments can produce a different tax result.
Trust and estate income Depends on the type of entitlement or receipt The relevant year depends on the type of trust or estate interest and whether the beneficiary receives, becomes entitled to or is treated as receiving the income. Use the trustees’ or personal representatives’ statement, normally form R185, and apply the beneficiary rules. The date the underlying asset paid the trust or estate is not always the beneficiary’s tax point.

Recognition rules for savings and dividend income

General recognition guide for an individual
Income source How the tax year is identified General recognition rule Important qualification
Savings and loan interest When interest arises Interest is generally charged for the tax year in which it arises. Receipt or credit to an account normally indicates that it has arisen. “Arising” can be wider than cash receipt, so the instrument’s terms and the individual’s entitlement must be checked.
Dividends and other distributions When paid or treated as paid UK dividends and other distributions are generally included in the tax year in which they are paid or treated as paid. The company’s accounting period does not determine the shareholder’s tax year. Apply the rule for the particular distribution.

When an income source starts during the tax year

A taxpayer who starts employment, begins trading, first lets a property or first becomes entitled to another taxable source part-way through 2026/27, for example, still uses the tax year ending 5 April 2027. Work through the following steps:

  1. Record the commencement event. Identify the first day of employment, trading or letting, or the date on which the legal entitlement or other charging event first arose.
  2. Identify every amount up to 5 April 2027. Do not assume that the first bank receipt is the commencement date.
  3. Apply the source-specific recognition rule. For a cash-basis business, follow qualifying receipts and payments. For an accruals-basis business, recognise amounts earned and incurred. For other income, apply the statutory receipt, entitlement, accrual or payment rule shown above.
  4. Carry later amounts into the next tax year where required. A cash-basis receipt after 5 April 2027 will normally fall into 2027/28, even if the invoice was issued before 6 April. Under the accruals basis, the same amount may belong to 2026/27 if it was earned by 5 April.
  5. Apply the annual tax rules. The Personal Allowance and rate bands are generally annual amounts and are not automatically reduced merely because an income source starts part-way through the year. Residence, death and other special rules must be considered separately.

Illustrative cash-basis trade: A sole trader begins business on 1 October 2026, issues an invoice on 30 March 2027 and receives payment on 10 April 2027. Assuming the cash basis applies and no special rule changes the result, that receipt falls into 2027/28. If the trader validly uses the accruals basis and the work was completed by 5 April 2027, the income may instead form part of the profit arising in 2026/27.

Illustrative property business: A landlord begins letting on 1 January 2027 and receives rent in advance before 5 April 2027 for a period extending beyond that date. Under the property cash basis, the receipt is generally brought into account when received. Under the accruals basis, the rent is allocated between the periods before and after 5 April according to the period to which it relates.
