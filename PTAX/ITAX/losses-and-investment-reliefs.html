Loss map

ITA 2007 Part 4 contains distinct regimes for trade losses, property-business losses, employment losses, share losses and miscellaneous losses. Possible destinations include current- or prior-year general income, profits of the same trade, terminal-period profits and, in specified cases, chargeable gains.

  • Claims have different time limits, ordering rules and restrictions.
  • Commerciality, hours worked, partnership status, overseas activity and tax-avoidance rules may restrict relief.
  • Many deductions from total income fall within the cap equal to the greater of £50,000 and 25% of adjusted total income; exceptions must be checked.

Investment reducers

EIS, SEIS and VCT relief operate under separate statutory conditions and generally reduce tax rather than income. Limits, qualifying-company tests, holding periods, carry-back and withdrawal events must be verified for the investment date. Share-loss relief may interact with EIS or SEIS relief already obtained.

Never select a loss claim solely by the largest immediate refund: claims can waste allowances, affect other years and consume losses irrevocably.

Sources
