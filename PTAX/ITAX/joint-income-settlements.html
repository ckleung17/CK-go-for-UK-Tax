Beneficial ownership

Income is normally taxed on the person beneficially entitled to it, subject to statutory attribution rules. Legal title, bank-account name and who spends the cash are evidence but are not always conclusive.

Spouses and civil partners

For spouses or civil partners living together, ITA 2007 s.836 generally treats income from jointly held property as split equally. A valid Form 17 declaration can apply the actual unequal beneficial split where the statutory conditions and evidence are satisfied. It is not an election for any preferred percentage and does not normally operate retrospectively.

Settlements

The settlements legislation may attribute income back to a settlor who retains an interest. Income from property provided by a parent for an unmarried minor child can be attributed to that parent where the statutory threshold is exceeded. Bare trusts, nominees, discretionary trusts and interest-in-possession trusts have different charging rules.

Transfers between spouses, dividend waivers, retained underlying assets and arrangements involving minor children are fact-sensitive. Cross-reference the Trust study note.

Sources
