Non-savings income

Common sources include:

  • Employment earnings and benefits: salary, wages, bonuses and taxable benefits arising from an office or employment.
  • Company directors’ remuneration: salary, fees, bonuses and taxable benefits arising from a directorship or employment with the company. Special rules determine when directors’ remuneration is treated as received.
  • Pensions and taxable state benefits: taxable payments from the State Pension, workplace or private pensions, together with state benefits specifically brought into charge.
  • Trading profits: the taxable profit of a trade, profession or vocation carried on by a sole trader, or an individual partner’s share of partnership profit.
  • Property income: the taxable profit arising from land or property, commonly through rent or licence fees.
  • Trust and estate income: income distributed to, or treated as received by, a beneficiary of a trust or a deceased person’s estate.
  • Miscellaneous income: amounts charged under the miscellaneous-income provisions when they are not taxed under another source, including certain annual payments and other income not otherwise charged.

Each source has its own charging, deduction and recognition rules before its figure reaches the overall Income Tax computation.

Official sources

  • Income Tax (Earnings and Pensions) Act 2003: employment, pension and social-security income provisions
  • Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005: principal income-source provisions
