Tax Appeals, Tribunals and Dispute Resolution

This study area begins when a person disputes an appealable tax decision and follows the available routes through HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC), the independent tribunals and higher courts. Closure notices, discovery assessments and information notices are principally covered in HMRC Enquiries, Assessments and Compliance Powers; they appear here only where they create an appeal, application or other procedural issue.

Status at 20 July 2026: the correct route depends on the legislation governing the particular decision. Direct-tax decisions generally require an appeal to HMRC before notification to the First-tier Tribunal, whereas many indirect-tax decisions may be appealed directly to the tribunal. Deadlines, payment rules, review rights and tribunal powers must therefore be checked against the decision notice and the applicable statute.

Each module will identify the decision, jurisdiction, governing provision, deadline and available remedy. An appeal, statutory review, complaint, Alternative Dispute Resolution and judicial review serve different purposes and are not substitutes unless the relevant law permits that route.

Sources checked 20 July 2026. Legislation and the tribunal procedure rules govern the formal process. Practice directions and case-management directions must also be followed.
