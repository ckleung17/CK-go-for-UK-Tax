HMRC Enquiries, Assessments and Compliance Powers

This study area covers how HMRC checks tax positions, obtains information, concludes enquiries, assesses unpaid tax and applies related safeguards and appeal rights. The governing procedure depends on the tax, return, notice, period and behaviour involved.

Coverage

  • Self Assessment enquiry windows;
  • Corporation Tax enquiries;
  • VAT and indirect-tax compliance checks;
  • closure notices and partial closure notices;
  • amendments during enquiries;
  • discovery assessments;
  • VAT assessments;
  • information notices under Schedule 36 to the Finance Act 2008;
  • taxpayer, third-party and financial-institution information notices;
  • inspections of premises and records;
  • data-gathering powers;
  • four-, six-, twelve- and twenty-year assessment time limits where applicable;
  • careless and deliberate behaviour;
  • offshore extensions;
  • penalties for inaccuracies and failures;
  • reasonable excuse and special reduction;
  • HMRC internal governance and statutory safeguards; and
  • appeals against assessments, notices and penalties.

Each module will distinguish the legislation from HMRC guidance and will identify the relevant tax, procedural conditions, time limits, taxpayer safeguards and appeal route.

Official starting points

  • Taxes Management Act 1970
  • Finance Act 1998, Schedule 18: Corporation Tax returns, enquiries and assessments
  • Finance Act 2008, Schedule 36: information and inspection powers
  • Value Added Tax Act 1994
  • HMRC Compliance Handbook
  • HMRC Enquiry Manual
  • HMRC compliance-check factsheets

Sources checked 20 July 2026. Legislation is the governing authority. HMRC manuals and factsheets describe HMRC’s interpretation and operational practice; they are not legislation. Detailed conclusions are fact-sensitive and require the provisions in force for the relevant tax and period.
