Sum of the Lower Proportions (SLP)

Scope: Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) applies to land transactions in England and Northern Ireland. This page explains the Schedule 15 Part 3 calculation for certain transfers from a partnership and reflects the law and HMRC guidance checked on 16 July 2026.

The sum of the lower proportions (SLP) is part of the statutory calculation in paragraphs 18 to 22 of Schedule 15 to the Finance Act 2003. It can apply when a partnership transfers a chargeable interest to a partner, a person connected with a partner, or another person within the rules.

This page does not cover transfers into a partnership or transfers of interests in a property-investment partnership. Those transactions have different Schedule 15 calculations.

The rules apply only where there is a partnership for SDLT purposes, the land is partnership property and the conditions in Schedule 15 are met. Joint ownership or a partnership deed alone does not necessarily establish a partnership carrying on a business.

The SDLT Charging Formula

Where Schedule 15 Part 3 applies, the legislation substitutes a market-value-based amount for the normal chargeable-consideration rules. Actual consideration and related debt are not used in this calculation.

Chargeable consideration = Market value × (100% − SLP%)

For a lease grant involving rent, paragraph 19 applies the same percentage separately to the relevant market value of the premium and to the rent’s net present value. The simplified formula above is illustrated with a freehold transfer.

  • If SLP is 100%, the deemed chargeable consideration under this formula is £0. Notification requirements and other relevant transactions or rules still need separate consideration.
  • If SLP is 40%, the deemed chargeable consideration is 60% of the chargeable interest’s market value at the effective date.

How SLP is calculated: the five statutory steps

Paragraph 20 prescribes the following process. HMRC explains its interpretation in SDLTM33750:

  1. Identify each relevant owner: a person who is entitled to a proportion of the chargeable interest immediately after the transaction and who, immediately before it, was a partner or connected with a partner.
  2. Identify the corresponding partner or partners for each relevant owner: immediately before the transaction, the person must have been a partner and must have been the relevant owner or an individual connected with that owner. If no relevant owner has a corresponding partner, the SLP is nil.
  3. Apportion the post-transaction interest: find each relevant owner’s proportion of the chargeable interest immediately after the transaction and apportion it among one or more of that owner’s corresponding partners. The legislation does not prescribe a single method of apportionment.
  4. Find each lower proportion: for every corresponding partner, compare the proportion of the chargeable interest attributed to that partner under Step 3 with the partnership share attributable to that partner. The lower figure is that partner’s lower proportion.
  5. Add the lower proportions: their total is the SLP.

For Schedule 15, a partner’s partnership share is generally their entitlement to the partnership’s income profits, but paragraphs 21 and 22 contain special rules for the share attributable to a partner. Earlier transfers of the property and whether the appropriate SDLT or Stamp Duty was paid can change the figure.

Illustrative example: transfer to one partner

Assume that, on 16 July 2026, a qualifying partnership transfers a freehold property with a market value of £2,000,000 to Partner A. Immediately before the transfer, A has a 30% partnership share, B has 30% and C has 40%. None of the partners is connected with another. Assume the property is partnership property, paragraph 18 applies and A’s attributable partnership share under paragraphs 21 and 22 is 30%.

SLP calculation

  • Step 1: A is the relevant owner because A acquires 100% of the property and was a partner immediately before the transaction.
  • Step 2: A is A’s only corresponding partner. B and C are not connected with A.
  • Step 3: A’s 100% post-transaction interest is apportioned to corresponding partner A.
  • Step 4: the lower of the 100% attributed interest and A’s 30% attributable partnership share is 30%.
  • Step 5: the SLP is 30%.
  • Deemed chargeable consideration: £2,000,000 × (100% − 30%) = £1,400,000.

The example determines chargeable consideration, not the SDLT payable. The applicable SDLT rates depend on the property’s classification, the purchaser and the wider facts. A land transaction return may still be required even where a calculation produces no tax.

Company incorporations

Where property is transferred from a partnership to a company, the company — not its shareholders — acquires the chargeable interest. The relevant-owner and corresponding-partner tests, including statutory connection and control rules, must be applied to the company and each partner. Matching the partners’ profit shares to their shareholdings in the company does not by itself establish the SLP.
