Leasehold transactions

Scope: SDLT applies to land transactions in England and Northern Ireland. This page reflects law and official guidance checked on 17 July 2026.

A lease transaction can produce separate SDLT calculations for a premium and for rent. Later assignments, variations, extensions, surrenders and renewals can also be land transactions or create further reporting duties.

Grant of a new lease

A premium is charged using the rates applicable to the property type. Rent under a new lease is converted to a net present value (NPV) under Schedule 5 Finance Act 2003. The premium and rent calculations are separate and should not simply be added together.

  • For a residential lease, rent NPV above the current residential NPV threshold is charged at the statutory residential rent rate.
  • For a non-residential or mixed lease, the rent NPV uses the separate non-residential rent bands.
  • Linked leases can affect how thresholds and rates are applied.
  • The current calculator and rates should be checked at the effective date.

A return can be required even when no tax is payable. The current general filing and payment deadline is 14 days after the effective date, subject to the statutory exceptions for non-notifiable transactions.

Assignments, surrenders and variations

Assignment

An assignee acquires the existing lease. SDLT is normally considered on the consideration for the assignment; the original rent NPV is not recalculated as though a fresh lease had been granted. Premium, debt and connected-transaction rules may still apply.

Surrender and regrant

An express replacement lease, an extension of the term or an extension of the demised premises can amount to a surrender and regrant. The new lease must then be tested as a new acquisition. A reduction in area, a rent-only variation or adding a tenant does not automatically have the same result.

Rent variation

An increase in rent outside the original lease terms during the first five years can be treated as the grant of a new lease for the additional rent. Variable or uncertain rent can also require a later recalculation or return when the amount is established.

Holding over, renewal and overlap relief

Where a tenant remains after a fixed term expires, Schedule 17A can treat the original lease as continuing for SDLT purposes and additional tax or reporting may arise. A later backdated renewal needs to be coordinated with the holding-over treatment.

Overlap relief can reduce rent counted for a replacement lease where substantially the same premises and periods overlap and rent for the earlier lease was already taken into account for SDLT. The relief cannot create a negative rent amount and does not apply merely because two leases concern related property.

Illustration

A business surrenders an existing shop lease and receives a longer lease of the same premises with increased rent and an additional floor.

  • Determine whether the transaction is a surrender and regrant.
  • Calculate any premium and the new rent NPV separately.
  • Identify rent and periods already taxed under the old lease.
  • Test overlap relief and the notification requirements.

Applied analysis: The deed label is not decisive. The original lease, variation, premises, effective date and rent history are required before the new lease calculation can be completed.

Practical checklist

  • Classify the property as residential, non-residential or mixed.
  • Identify premium, rent, term, break clauses, rent-free periods and VAT.
  • Check assignments, variations and extensions for a new land transaction.
  • Reconcile holding-over periods and replacement leases.
  • Retain the NPV calculation and evidence for any overlap relief.
