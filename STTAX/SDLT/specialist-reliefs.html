Specialist SDLT reliefs

Scope: SDLT applies to land transactions in England and Northern Ireland. This page is a study map, not an exhaustive substitute for the legislation, and reflects law and official guidance checked on 17 July 2026.

Specialist reliefs generally require a land transaction return and an express claim even if the relief reduces SDLT to nil. Each regime has its own purchaser, property, purpose, timing, withdrawal and anti-avoidance conditions.

Residential and housing reliefs

Property traders, house builders and employers

Schedule 6A can relieve specified acquisitions of dwellings by house-building companies, property traders or employers, including qualifying chain-break, probate and employee-relocation situations. Conditions can restrict occupation, letting, refurbishment and land area, and later events can withdraw relief.

Right to Buy and social housing

Schedule 9 provides special treatment for qualifying Right to Buy and shared-ownership transactions. Section 71 can exempt specified acquisitions by qualifying registered social-housing providers where the purchaser and funding or transfer conditions are met.

Business and corporate reliefs

Group, reconstruction and acquisition relief

Schedule 7 contains separate reliefs for qualifying intra-group transfers and corporate reconstructions or acquisitions. Group membership, beneficial entitlement, arrangements for change of control, commercial purpose and later degrouping events require detailed review. See the companies and partnerships page for the group-relief foundation.

Sale and leaseback

Section 57A can exempt the leaseback element of a qualifying sale-and-leaseback arrangement. The sale remains subject to the exchange and consideration rules, and the statutory conditions exclude specified connected corporate and pre-completion arrangements.

Development and public-interest reliefs

Separate provisions cover compulsory purchase facilitating development, compliance with planning obligations, transfers involving public bodies, qualifying charities, national-purpose bodies and collective enfranchisement by leaseholders. The identity and purpose of the purchaser, the statutory route and the use of the land are central.

Charities relief can be withdrawn if the property ceases to be held for qualifying charitable purposes or disqualifying arrangements apply. A charity-law status or registration label does not alone establish the SDLT relief.

Alternative finance and special tax sites

Alternative property finance

Sections 71A–73 can prevent multiple SDLT charges where a financial institution acquires land and leases or resells it to the customer under a qualifying alternative finance arrangement. The first acquisition, later exempt steps and any termination or variation must follow the statutory structure.

Freeports and Investment Zones

Schedule 6C can relieve qualifying acquisitions in designated special tax sites where the land is intended and actually used in a qualifying manner throughout the control period. Under current legislation and designations, qualifying acquisition windows can run to 30 September 2031 for Freeport tax sites and 30 September 2034 for Investment Zone tax sites. Site designation, use, apportionment and withdrawal must be checked at the effective date.

Investment-fund and other specialist regimes

Further regimes include property authorised investment fund and co-ownership authorised contractual scheme seeding relief, acquisitions by certain national bodies, demutualisations and limited-liability partnership incorporations. These are specialist transactional provisions and should be analysed directly from the legislation and current HMRC guidance rather than from a general relief summary.

Illustration

A company acquires land in a designated Freeport tax site, plans a qualifying trade and expects to transfer part of the site within its group.

  • Confirm the site and acquisition date are within the designation.
  • Map qualifying and non-qualifying intended uses.
  • Test Freeport relief and group relief independently.
  • Diarise the control period, use changes and possible withdrawal returns.

Applied analysis: A relief code is the end of the analysis, not the beginning. The transaction file must evidence every condition and monitor later clawback events.

Practical checklist

  • Identify the precise statutory relief before completion.
  • Confirm purchaser, property, purpose, funding and timing conditions.
  • Check anti-avoidance and arrangements restrictions.
  • Claim relief on the correct return.
  • Maintain a withdrawal and control-period diary.
