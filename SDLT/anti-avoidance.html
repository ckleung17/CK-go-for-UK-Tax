SDLT anti-avoidance

Scope: SDLT applies to land transactions in England and Northern Ireland. This page reflects law and official guidance checked on 17 July 2026.

The ordinary charging rules, linked-transaction provisions and relief conditions should be applied first. Sections 75A–75C Finance Act 2003 can then replace the result of a series of transactions with a notional land transaction where the statutory conditions are met.

Section 75A framework

Broadly, section 75A considers whether one person disposes of a chargeable interest, another person acquires it or derives it, a number of transactions are involved in connection with that disposal and acquisition, and the total SDLT otherwise payable is less than the amount on the statutory notional transaction.

The analysis identifies the vendor, purchaser, scheme transactions, incidental transactions, chargeable interest, consideration and effective date prescribed by sections 75A–75C. It is not enough to compare the first and last conveyances without applying each definition.

Section 75A has no tax-avoidance main-purpose condition. HMRC’s guidance, reflecting Project Blue Ltd v HMRC [2018] UKSC 30, describes an objective test: a tax saving resulting from the series can be sufficient regardless of the parties’ motive.

Other anti-avoidance rules

Many reliefs contain their own commercial-purpose, change-of-control, withdrawal or tax-advantage restrictions. Pre-completion relief, group relief, charity relief, higher-rate exclusions and special tax-site relief must each be tested under their own provisions before any broader section 75A conclusion.

Linked transactions under section 108 are not the same as section 75A. Transactions can be linked without section 75A applying, and section 75A can require a wider review of transactions and persons.

Disclosure of Tax Avoidance Schemes

The Disclosure of Tax Avoidance Schemes (DOTAS) regime can require disclosure of an SDLT proposal or arrangement where the statutory benefit and prescribed-description tests are met. The promoter normally has the primary disclosure obligation, but users can have obligations in specified cases. A scheme reference number does not approve the arrangements or settle the SDLT treatment.

Illustration

A purchaser uses several companies, contracts and financing steps to acquire a property, and the returns show less SDLT than a direct acquisition.

  • Apply the ordinary charge and any claimed relief to each step.
  • Identify every transaction involved in the disposal and acquisition.
  • Test sections 75A–75C objectively, including the notional consideration.
  • Consider DOTAS and any relief-specific anti-avoidance rule separately.

Applied analysis: Commercial motivation does not by itself prevent section 75A applying. The complete documents, flows of value and return positions must be reviewed before implementation.

Practical checklist

  • Draw the legal steps and cash or debt flows.
  • Identify connected persons and linked transactions.
  • Record commercial evidence without treating purpose as the section 75A test.
  • Check every relief condition and withdrawal event.
  • Obtain specialist advice on section 75A, DOTAS and return disclosure.
