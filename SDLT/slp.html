Sum of the Lower Proportions (SLP)

The Sum of the Lower Proportions (SLP) is a statutory tax formula under Schedule 15 of the Finance Act 2003 used to calculate Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT) when property moves between partnerships and connected parties (like partners or limited companies).

Instead of charging SDLT on the full market value of the property, the SLP formula measures how much of the property's ultimate ownership remains with the same economic owners throughout the transfer. SDLT is only charged on the percentage of the property effectively "sold" to outside or new parties.

The SDLT Charging Formula

When calculating tax for these transactions, HMRC completely ignores actual money paid or mortgage debt taken over. Instead, they calculate a deemed chargeable consideration using this calculation:

Chargeable Consideration = Market Value × (100% - SLP%)
  • If SLP is 100%, the calculation becomes Market Value × 0% = £0, meaning no SDLT is due.
  • If SLP is 40%, the calculation becomes Market Value × 60%, meaning SDLT is charged on 60% of the property's current value.

How SLP is Calculated (The 5-Step Process)

To find the final SLP percentage, HMRC uses a five-step tracking process (SDLTM33750):

  1. Identify the "Relevant Owners": Who owns the property after the transaction, and was a partner (or connected to a partner) before it? (e.g., The shareholders of the new company).
  2. Identify the "Corresponding Partners": For each relevant owner, who was the matching partner in the partnership before the transfer happened?
  3. Apportion the Shares: Look at the percentage of the property the relevant owner holds after the transaction, and match it against their original partnership income profit share before the transaction.
  4. Isolate the "Lower Proportion": For each person, compare their percentage ownership before vs. their percentage ownership after. Take the lowest of those two numbers.
  5. Sum the Proportions: Add all those individual lower proportions together to get your final SLP.

Working Example: Incorporating a 50/50 Partnership

Imagine Partner A and Partner B run a partnership. They share profits 50/50 and transfer a property portfolio worth £2,000,000 into a new limited company.

Scenario 1: Ownership matches perfectly (50/50 shares)

  • Partner A: Share before = 50% | Share after = 50%. The lower proportion is 50%.
  • Partner B: Share before = 50% | Share after = 50%. The lower proportion is 50%.
  • SLP Total: 50% + 50% = 100%.
  • Tax Result: £2,000,000 × (100% - 100%) = £0 SDLT due.

Scenario 2: An outside investor is brought into the new company

They transfer the property to a new company where Partner A gets 40%, Partner B gets 40%, and a new Investor gets 20%.

  • Partner A: Share before = 50% | Share after = 40%. The lower proportion is 40% (the lower number).
  • Partner B: Share before = 50% | Share after = 40%. The lower proportion is 40%.
  • New Investor: Share before = 0% | Share after = 20%. The lower proportion is 0%.
  • SLP Total: 40% + 40% + 0% = 80%.
  • Tax Result: The company pays SDLT on 20% of the market value (100% - 80%). The taxable consideration is £400,000 (£2,000,000 × 20%).
